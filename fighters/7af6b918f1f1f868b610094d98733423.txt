<strong>Meaning of Monte’s Nickname:</strong> Montanha means “Mountain” in Portuguese, the name was given by Cesar and Ralph Gracie when Monte started training, mainly because he was a big man (6’4” and 235lbs).<br />
.<br />
<strong>Monte Egbert’s favourite Technique/Position:</strong> Inverted Guard.<br />
.<br />
<strong>Monte Egbert’s Weight Category:</strong> Pesado (Heavyweight)<br />
.<br />
<strong>Monte Egbert’s Main Achievements:</strong> Silver Medalist at the 1997 US Nationals (Joe Moreira Tournament); Silver Medalist 2005 US open (on the day of his 50th Birthday) on the Masters Division, fighting opponents almost  20 years younger).<br />
.<br />
<strong>Monte Egbert’s in Jiu Jitsu:</strong> Cesar Gracie Jiu Jitsu – Team Gracie Fighter.</p>

<h2>Monte Egbert Biography</h2>
<p>Monte Egbert was born on the 13th of October 1955 at Pleasanton,  State of California – United States of America. </p>
<p>Monte started his Jiu Jitsu tutelage later than most. He had played Basketball throughout most his life from grade school to HS, College and the Adult City Leagues, but broke his foot at the age of 38 and had to retire from the sport. His body still craved for a strong cardio workout, and jiu jitsu was the chosen sport that fitted the big man’s needs, and so at the tender age of 39 he started training in the gentle art.</p>
<p>Montanha had trained on and off in Judo for many years as his father is a Kodokan Judoka Black Belt, Monte also trained Jet Kwon Do and Boxing and recently picked up the Filipino Martial Art of Cali.</p>

<p>In 2003, Montanha wrote and released the book, &#8220;the techniques of Brazilian Jiu-jitsu&#8221; just after Renzo and Royler released their book. His book’s drawings were all made by Monte himself, as he is a tattoo artist. The book is named “the Techniques of Brazilian Jiu-jitsu” and can be bought from Amazon.</p>
<p>Montanha achieved his Blue belt on Jan 30 1996 from Ralph Gracie, his Purple belt, Aug 4 1998 off the hands of late Ryan Gracie and his Brown belt, on Nov 25 2006 from Cesar Gracie. Mr Egbert has ran and taught at Cesar Gracie&#8217;s Berkeley Academy from 2001 to 2003. And in the years since he has taught a couple days a week in between main instructors or whenever someone is on vacation he fills in. His love for jiu jitsu and it&#8217;s culture has only grown in these 15 years of training.</p>
