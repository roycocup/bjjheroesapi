<strong>Armando Wridt’s Nickname:</strong> “Dedinho” was a name given by his training partners at Gracie Jiu Jitsu. It stands for “Little Finger”. The Nickname was given as Mr Wridt is missing his right thumb; he lost it while working with a wood cutter as a teenager.<br />
.<br />
<strong>Armando Wridt’s Main Achievements in (BJJ/Grappling/MMA):</strong> being born in a time where No Holds Barred fights were the way to showcase jiu jitsu, Armando fought 3 fights winning all by first round submission.</p>

<h2>Armando Wridt Biography</h2>
<p>Armando Wridt met Helio Gracie when he was 21 years old; he was in the military service and played Basketball for his regiment. Helio and he had a friend in common and Armando started visiting the Gracie Farm regularly picking up Jiu Jitsu training with the Gracie brotherhood.</p>
<p>Finishing his obligatory Military Service time, he turned to the Gracies for shelter and ended living in the Gracie Academy for 8 years.</p>
<p>During this time, Armando fought for the academy on 3 occasions at Vale-Tudo (no-holds-barred) events in Rio de Janeiro, although fighting was not in his nature. Armando was a true fan of “Mahatma” Gandhi, and he does not believe in violence, however, to please his masters he fought and finished all three fights with a quick submission.</p>
<p>After spending 8 years living and breathing the Gracie way, Armando left the academy (with Helio’s consent) and started teaching to personal body guards at the ESNI (Escola de Serviço Nacional de Informação).<br />
Today he is retired and lives off the land in his own farm in the outskirts of Brasília, capital of Brazil.</p>
<p>Armando Wridt is one of the very restricted group of people to have received the Red Belt off the hands of Gracie Jiu Jitsu Founder Helio Gracie.</p>
