						</div><p><div id="attachment_356" class="wp-caption alignright" style="width: 210px"><a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/03/Jacare-Romero-Cavalcanti.jpg"><img src="http://www.bjjheroes.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/03/Jacare-Romero-Cavalcanti.jpg" alt="Jacare Romero Cavalcanti" title="Jacare-Romero-Cavalcanti" width="200" height="240" class="size-full wp-image-356" /></a><p class="wp-caption-text">Jacare Romero Cavalcanti</p></div>Romero “Jacare” Cavalcanti is one of the best instructors in Brazilian Jiu Jitsu history, he has coached hundreds of fighters and produced some of the best the World has to offer in Grappling. he is one of the founders of Team Alliance and today he resides in the USA.<br />
.</p>
<h2>Romero Cavalcanti in Detail</h2>
<p><strong>Nickname:</strong> Jacaré means Alligator in Portuguese. The name was given to him by his beach soccer buddys, although it is unclear why the name was given.<br />
.<br />
<strong>Lineage:</strong> Mitsuyo Maeda > <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/carlos-gracie-sr-profile">Carlos Gracie</a> > <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/helio-gracie">Helio Gracie</a> > <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/rolls-gracie-profile">Rolls Gracie</a> > Romero Cavalcanti<br />
.<br />
<strong>Main Achievements in BJJ:</strong> Although Romero Cavalcanti did compete much in Jiu Jitsu in the beginning of his career and got most of his credit in the Jiu Jitsu world .<br />
.<br />
<strong>Team/Association:</strong> Alliance Team.</p>
<h3>Jacare Romero cavalcanti Biography</h3>
<p>Romero Cavalcanti was born on October 17, 1952 in Recife, Brazil and moved at a very early age to Rio de Janeiro. His first Jiu Jitsu coach was Jaildo Gomes (the brother of famous Vale Tudo fighter from the early days, Ivan Gomes), Romero was in his early teens and stayed with Gomes until he was 18 years old, at this time he moved camps to Carlson Gracie’s academy affiliate which was ran by a brown belt named “Toninho”. It was only when &#8220;Jacare&#8221; Romero achieved his blue belt that “Toninho” started taking him to meet and train with the Gracie with the purpose of Toughening him up. </p>
<p>In 1972, “Jacare” moved to New York to study, and abandoned Jiu Jitsu completely until the end of 1974 when he returned to Rio. During Romero’s stay in New York <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/rolls-gracie-profile">Rolls Gracie</a> visited and he got to know the Gracie Legend up close.<br />
When Romero returned to Rio de Janeiro in late 1974, he restarted his BJJ training with <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/rolls-gracie-profile">Rolls Gracie</a>, the two had established a friendship and Rolls was considered the best teacher and competitor of his time, so the choice was more than understandable.</p>
<p>With Rolls Gracie’s teachings, Cavalcanti’s jiu jitsu developed in heaps and bounds, Rolls was innovative and a perfect role model to which Romero got very close to and considered a fatherly figure. In training with this new academy he trained with many of the great’s from his time, people like <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/carlos-carlinhos-gracie-jr">Carlos Gracie Junior</a>, <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/rickson-gracie-facts-and-bio">Rickson Gracie</a>, Mauricio Gomes, Paulo Conde, Ricardo Azoury and many others.</p>
<p>In 1982 Jacare Romero was awarded his Black Belt by the hands of Rolls himself, being the last Black Belt ever awarded by Rolls, the last of a very strict group of 5. After the tragic death of his mentor, Rolls Gracie (in 1982) <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/rickson-gracie-facts-and-bio">Carlos Gracie Junior</a> (Rolls younger brother) picked up the class and the gym, but not for long as Carlos had plans to open his own academy in the Barra da Tijuca neighbourhood. As Romero witnessed the terminus of his academy, he chose to start training with Rickson Gracie at his academy in Humaita area of Rio de Janeiro. </p>
<p>After a run at the Gracie academy in Humaita, Romero decided to open up his own place, and so Jacare Jiu Jitsu Academy was opened to the public. In 1992 A few years after Jacare Jiu Jitsu was opened, a new academy was created. The academy had Mr Jacare Romero at the helm, and several of his very own Black Belts as his right hand men, a team that grew stronger each year.</p>
<p>Jacare Romero decided to move to the US to broaden the horizon of his new team in 1995, first in Miami and finally settling in Atlanta. In 2009, a few months after being awarded the “On The Mat” Instructor of the Year award, Romero Cavalcanti was admitted at a Hospital in Altlanta with a serious illness. He has since left the Hospital and is recovered.</p>
<p>Jacare Cavalcanti is today one of the most highly regarded “Mestres” of BJJ in the World, with a legion of quality Black Belts under him, and Alliance Team is one of the big sharks in BJJ at a World stage.</p>
