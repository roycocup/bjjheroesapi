<strong>Full name:</strong> Thiago Jorge Borges<br />
.<br />
<strong>Meaning of Thiago Borges&#8217;s Nickname:</strong> Monstro is how Thiago is most commonly called. The name was given because of his size (Monstro walks around at 275lbs). Monstro means Monster in portuguese.<br />
.<br />
<strong>Thiago Monstro Borges Lineage:</strong> Mitsuyo Maeda > <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/carlos-gracie-sr-profile">Carlos Gracie</a> > <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/helio-gracie">Helio Gracie</a> > <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/rickson-gracie-facts-and-bio">Rickson Gracie</a> > Marcelo Bering > Valdomiro  Junior > Milton Maximiano > <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/carlos-portugues-vieira-wiki-bio">Carlos Vieira</a> > Thiago Borges<br />
.<br />
<strong>Thiago Monstro Borges Main Achievements in Grappling:</strong> 4x Rio de Janeiro State Champion (1999, 2000, 2001, 2002); 3x Sao Paulo State Champion (2003, 2004, 2005); 2x Brasilia State Champion; 3x Sao Paulo International Champion (2004, 2005, 2006) and 1x Open Weigh Champion(2005); Open Weight European Champion (2009 &#8211; CBJJE); Silver Medallist at the ADCC Brazilian Trials (2005); Submission Wrestling de Campos Champion, 2x Luta Livre State Champion<br />
.<br />
<strong>Thiago Borges&#8217;s Favourite Technique/Position:</strong> Guard Pass<br />
.<br />
<strong>Thiago &#8220;Monstro&#8221; Borges Weight Division:</strong> Pesadissimo<br />
.<br />
<strong>Thiago Borges Team/Association:</strong> Checkmat</p>
<h2>Thiago Monstro Borges Biography</h2>
<p>Thiago Borges was born on the 29th of April 1982 in Vitória, State of Espirito Santo, though he moved to Rio de Janeiro still young.</p>
<p>He decided to start  training training Jiu Jitsu after watching the very first UFC&#8217;s when Royce Gracie was winning against everyone. There was a gym close to his house where some of his friends trained, and as he felt he was no good at football BJJ seemed like the right sport to practice. He joined that same gym which his friends attended, there he met his first instructor and a man who would become a very close friend, Huanderson Pavao from Gama Filho&#8217;s team (now GTF &#8211; Grappling Fight Team).</p>
<p>He stayed with Pavao for many years going through the ranks from white to brown belt, competing every chance he could in jiu jitsu, Judo and luta livre, a style of submission wrestling common in Brazil as well as submission wrestling.  This partnership with Mr Huanderson would come to an end as Thiago moved to Brazilia in 2002. There he began training with <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/carlos-portugues-vieira-wiki-bio">Carlos Eduardo Vieira</a> (also known as &#8220;Portugues&#8221;) an instructor from the Cia Paulista. &#8220;Monstro&#8221; and &#8220;Portugues&#8221; already knew each other from before, Thiago often travelled to the Brazilian capital to visit his girlfriend (today his wife) and he would always bring his Gi with him to train at Vieira&#8217;s gym.</p>
<p>After 2 years in Brazilia Monstro received his black belt (December 2004). Shortly after, in 2005, Thiago moved to Europe to seek out different challenges, there he met Rodrigo Cabral and Eduardo Azevedo, two black belts from the Brasa Jiu Jitsu team who were forming their own team in London, England. The two invited him to be part of their team to which Thiago accepted. A few years later the Brasa team would come to a split in it&#8217;s management, as Eduardo, Rodrigo and Monstro were loyal to <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/leo-vieira-facts-and-bio">Leonardo Vieira</a> who had also split from Brasa to form Checkmat, the trio decided to form alliance with Vieira.</p>
<p>Today Monstro teaches and competes in the old continent were he added another style of competition to his resume, the ever present MMA (mixed martial arts).</p>
