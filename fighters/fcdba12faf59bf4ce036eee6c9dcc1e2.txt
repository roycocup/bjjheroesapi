</div>								<h1 class="post-title"><a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/caio-terra" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to Caio Terra">Caio Terra</a></h1>
								<div style="padding-top:0px;padding-right:0px;padding-bottom:0px;padding-left:0px;;">
							<iframe

						</div><div id="attachment_598" class="wp-caption alignright" style="width: 236px"><a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/Caio-Terra.jpg"><img class="size-medium wp-image-598" title="Caio-Terra" src="http://www.bjjheroes.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/Caio-Terra-226x300.jpg" alt="Caio Terra BJJ" width="226" height="300" /></a><p class="wp-caption-text">Caio Terra</p></div>
<p>Caio Terra is a world Jiu Jitsu champion who fights for Gracie Elite Team. The Brazilian black belt has had a controvertial career in BJJ, with a bumpy start at his first academy (Checkmat), a rampant rise to black belt unlike BJJ&#8217;s usual standards and constant battles with Jiu Jitsu&#8217;s refereeing. But throughout the polemical progress in BJJ Caio Terra&#8217;s talent and track record make him one of the best and most fighters of his generation.<br />
.</p>
<h2>Caio Terra in Detail</h2>
<p><strong>Lineage:</strong> Mitsuyo Maeda &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/carlos-gracie-sr-profile">Carlos Gracie Sr.</a> &gt; Reylson Gracie &gt; Paulo Mauricio Strauch &gt; Caio Terra<br />
.<br />
<strong>Caio Terra&#8217;s Main Titles in Jiu Jitsu (BJJ):</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>World Black Belt Champion (2008);</li>
<li>3x World No Gi Champion (2009, 2008, 2010)</li>
<li>3x Mundials Silver Medallist (World Championships &#8211; 2010, 2009, 2005 blue belt)</li>
<li>Pan American Silver Medal (2010)</li>
<li>No Gi World Championship Silver Medal (2008)</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Weight Division:</strong> Galo (Rooster Weight) 57kg<br />
.<br />
<strong>Association/Team:</strong> Cesar Gracie/Gracie Elite Team</p>
<h3>Caio Terra&#8217;s Biography</h3>
<p>Caio Terra was born on 1986 in Rio de Janeiro Brazil. Caio Terra&#8217;s start in Jiu Jitsu (in 2003) did not look promising, he was forced to train BJJ by his mother after a series of bullying issues at school. Caio was a very small teenager weighing around 85 pounds at the time, and his mother felt he needed to train a martial art in order to learn how to defend himself. As Caio refused to train his mother joined the class forcing her teenage son to tag along.</p>
<p>The gym chosen by his mother was the famous Fight Zone academy in Copacabana, led by <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/rodrigo-comprido-medeiros">Rodrigo Medeiros</a> and <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/ricardo-ricardinho-vieira">Ricardo Vieira</a>. But although Caio had two World Champions working by his side, he did not believe in Jiu Jitsu and much rather spend his time playing football (soccer) with his friends. Fortunately Caio Terra did not quit as he believed he owed to his mother as much as to give it a shot. The persistence paid off and he received his blue belt having never competed and still not fully aware that this was the sport he wanted to follow.</p>
<p>After Caio received his blue belt, his coaches and colleagues started encouraging him to compete, and so he did. Although he did not have much faith in himself he managed to win his first fight, after which he was so nervous he asked his coach to quit, he did not want to enter the mat again, but his coach insisted and so he fought again&#8230; and won again! One by one he fought and won until he made it to the final. His final match was against a man that would become his nemesis in the black belt division later in his life, <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/bruno-malfacine-bjj-wiki">Bruno Malfacine</a>. The fight against the Gama Filho fighter (today with Alliance) was a back and forth battle but in the end Malfacine managed to submit Caio Terra. After this roller coaster of emotions Caio was convinced that BJJ was something he wanted to do seriously and he started training every day, twice a day.</p>
<p>Caio Terra&#8217;s progress was clearly visible with this new found enthusiasm and dedication. In 2004 he fought at the Mundials (World Championships) as a blue belt and managed to place second &#8211; losing by one advantage. Unfortunately shortly after the championship (In August 2004), Caio had an argument with his coach (Vieira) causing him to leave the gym. He found a new home at Paulo Strauch&#8217;s academy.</p>
<p>Around this time he also decided to make a sparring mat area at his home, he took down a few walls and built it himself with the help of some friends. This arena would serve as the big laboratory for Caio&#8217;s rocket fast rise to black belt. His house was used as an open mat area for whoever wanted to come by and roll or drill Jiu Jitsu. Caio would get visits all day, some times late at night, but being a good host and always eager to train Caio would open his doors.</p>
<p>Caio received his black belt from Reylson Gracie and Paulo Strauch on July 2006 making his rise to black belt one of the fastest ever recorded, still, there was no doubt Caio deserved his belt as he had successively reached the podium of a World tournament for 3 years in a row (2005 Silver Medal Blue Belt, 2006 Bronze Medal Purple Belt, 2007 Silver Medal Brown Belt).</p>
<p>On Caio&#8217;s first year as a Black Belt, he reached the top spots of the World Championship again, this time achieving what had been missing throughout his career, the gold medal.</p>
<p>Caio Terra&#8217;s career as a black belt has not been without it&#8217;s ups and downs. Although Caio has been successful at achieving podiums in pretty much every tournament he decides to compete in, he has been unlucky when it comes to refereeing decisions, being the most controversial his final of the Pan American in 2010 against the Check Mat fighter &#8220;Esquisito&#8221; Holanda Vieira.</p>
<p>Though Caio Terra&#8217;s mother stopped training after the first few months at Fight Zone, Caio&#8217;s brother Kim Terra continued fighting and training and is today part of the Checkmat team.</p>
<p>As a thank you note I would like to mention <a href="http://www.insidebjj.com/">Inside BJJ</a>&#8216;s fantastic interview with Caio Terra which provided most of the content here described and our friend <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/monte-egbert-montanha-wiki">Monte Egbert</a> for his valuable insight.</p>
