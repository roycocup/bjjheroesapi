<h2>Helvecio Penna Biography</h2>
<p>Helvecio Penna Neto was born on 1961 in Brazil. His first contact with martial arts was at the age of 5, by his mother’s influence who wanted him to do some sort of physical activity. He became a successful competitor in the Japanese national sport winning several Rio de Janeiro Judo State Championships, fighting also at a world stage for the Brazilian National Judo team.</p>
<p>He sought out Jiu Jitsu for the first time when he was 31. He started BJJ to try and distance himself from some of the other Judoka’s by sharpening his ground game, but ended up falling in love with the Brazilian style of grappling. At age 42 he received his black belt by the hands of one of the greatest fighters of all times, Mr Ricardo de la Riva. He went on to fight at the “Internacional Masters e Seniors”, the greatest tournament in the World for those divisions, and he won it 5 years in a row. He missed 2009 due to a knee injury, but he came back strong in 2010, winning the event once again.</p>
<p>Helvecio Penna is an extremely active individual, he is a teacher of sports education at a private school in Rio de Janeiro, he also teaches Judo at a social project in Rio de Janeiro for the impoverished, but this busy schedule does not detour him from his BJJ training, as he comes to practice religiously every day at 7am.</p>
<p>Though Helvecio was a “made name” in the Master and Senior’s divisions, it wasn’t until 2006 that his name made the headlines of Jiu Jitsu magazines for his fighting prowess in the Adults division. At the Mundials (World Championships) Helvecio made it to the Quarter Finals where he fought the heavy favourite <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/xande-ribeiro-fighter-wiki">Alexandre Ribeiro</a>. The hard fought battle was 0&#215;0 on the score boards until around the 8th minute when Helvecio gassed and succumbed to “Xande’s” triangle. Ribeiro would later on defeat <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/roger-gracie-bio">Roger Gracie</a> in the final.</p>
<p>In 2010 Helvecio achieved 1st place at the Rio de Janeiro State Trials at the age of 49 in the adult division.</p>
