<p>Robert Drysdale is an American Grappler, a Brazilian Jiu Jitsu World Champion and ADCC Open Weight Division Champion. He has also fought in Mixed Martial Arts (MMA) and has a BJJ School in the Las Vegas.<br />
.</p>
<h2>Robert Drysdale in Detail</h2>
<p><strong>Lineage:</strong> Mitsuyo Maeda &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/carlos-gracie-sr-profile">Carlos Gracie</a> &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/helio-gracie">Helio Gracie</a> &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/rolls-gracie-profile">Rolls Gracie</a> &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/romero-jacare-cavalcanti-wiki">Romero Cavalcanti</a> &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/leo-vieira-facts-and-bio">Leonardo Vieira</a> &gt; Robert Drysdale<br />
.<br />
<strong>Main Achievements (BJJ/ Submission Wrestling):</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>World Champion (Black Belt Division 2005);</li>
<li>2x World Cup Champion (Black Belt 2005, 2006);</li>
<li>2x World Silver Medallist (Black Belt 2006, 2007);</li>
<li>ADCC Absolute (Open Weight) Champion 2007</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Weight Category (BJJ):</strong> Super-Pesado (Super Heavy Weight) Under 100Kg-221Lbs<br />
.<br />
<strong>Favourite Jiu Jitsu Position/Move:</strong> Omoplata (Shoulder Blade Lock) and Triangle.<br />
.<br />
<strong>Association/Team:</strong> Brasa for the most part of his Black Belt career, he now runs his own academy as Robert Drysdale Brazilian Jiu Jitsu.</p>
<h3>Robert Drysdale Biography</h3>
<p>Robert Drysdale was born in Provo on the State of Utah, USA in 1981. He moved with his family to Brazil when he was just 6 years old, and began training Jiu Jitsu well in his teens (he was 17) at the “Quatro Tempos” Academy on the city of “Itú” State of Sao Paulo, Brazil.</p>
<p>He was still a white belt when he had to move back to the US to pursue his academic studies at a University in Las Vegas. Having fallen in love with BJJ, he looked up for a Jiu Jitsu academy and ended up training with instructor Steve da Silva. Steve was the first teacher to really believe in Drysdale’s potential. With his instructors encouragement Robert decided then to move back to Brazil were the sport was more professionalized.</p>
<p>The year was 2000 and Robert Drysdale packed his bags and moved to Indaiatuba in Brazil. He first started training at the “Maromba” academy with Professor Paulo Streckert. In the “Maromba” Academy, Drysdale fully dedicated himself to BJJ and competition. With his full atention in the sport he started picking up the benefits of his hard work, achieving many podiums and earning himself a World Title in the Purple Belt Division, and receiving his Brown Belt.</p>
<p>He then met and befriended Demian Maia who invited him to go and train at the mighty Brasa academy then led by the Alliance &#8220;stray&#8221; stars who had formed this new academy. After Robert received a “beating” in training by Leonardo Vieira, he decided this should be the place to train. He asked his master Paulo Streckert and they both agreed it was a wise choice, and so began Robert Drysdale association with the Brasa Team.<br />
In late 2007 after winning the ADCC in the Open Weight Division, Robert Drysdale moved back to the USA. First to run the grappling classes at Extreme Couture in Las Vegas, and later moving on to open his own grappling academy.</p>
<p>On October 2008 Drysdale ventured in the MMA world, fighting at an Amateur competiton called TUFF-N-UFF. He defeated his oponent by Armbar in the first round.</p>
