In the late 1950&#8242;s and 1960&#8242;s he was a fierce Gracie Academy competitor. Having a Judo and Power lifting background gave him physical strength that was famed in Rio de Janeiro in those days. Today the retired Judo black belt and BJJ red belt is regarded as a living encyclopaedia of both MMA and BJJ.<br />
.<br />
<strong>Full Name:</strong> Oswaldo Gomes da Rosa<br />
.<br />
<strong>Nickname:</strong> &#8220;Paquetá&#8221; was a nickname given to Mr Oswaldo, it is the name of the place where he used to live and everybody called him by &#8220;The Fighter from Paquetá&#8221;. With time that was shortened to plain &#8220;Paquetá&#8221;.<br />
.<br />
<strong>Oswaldo Paqueta&#8217;s Lineage:</strong> Mitsuyo Maeda > <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/carlos-gracie-sr-profile">Carlos Gracie</a> > <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/carlson-gracie-profile">Carlson Gracie</a> > Oswaldo &#8220;Paqueta&#8221;<br />
.<br />
<strong>Oswaldo Paqueta&#8217;s Association/Team:</strong> Academia Gracie/Carlson Gracie</p>
<h2>Oswaldo Paqueta Biography</h2>
<p>Oswaldo Paqueta was born on the 8th of January 1940. He was raised in a very humble environment, his father was a street sweeper and money was scarce. Consequently Oswaldo found himself working to help the family from a very early age. </p>
<p>Even though life presented these obstacles, Paqueta always found time to exercise. The first sports he took seriously were bodybuilding and Judo, two sports in which he excelled. The Judo he learned from a Japanese instructor called Nagashima who had a Dojo in Rio de Janeiro. </p>
<p>A while after he began Judo he started hearing about Jiu Jitsu and decided to give that a try as well, his first instructor was Almir Ribeiro in 1958. Fate had it that he would find a job as a messenger at an office allocated in the same building as the &#8220;Academia Gracie&#8221; the famous Jiu Jitsu gym led by <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/helio-gracie">Helio Gracie</a>. Paquetá would often use his lunchbreak to visit the Gracie Academy and see the classes though he couldn&#8217;t afford to train there. With the time spent at the famed Jiu Jitsu academy he began a friendship with <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/carlson-gracie-profile">Carlson Gracie</a> the nephew of Helio who trained there, the bond between the two grew and one day Carlson asked his uncle Helio Gracie if he could let Paquetá train there, Helio agreed and as Paqueta could not afford the tuition they settled to have him there for a fraction of the standard monthly fee. </p>
<p>Paqueta was always regarded as a very strong individual in fact the only person stronger then him at the gym (according to Oswaldo himself) was Carlson who would rip a Yellow Pages book in half in a display of his grip strength on occasion. Recognizing the hard man in Oswaldo, Carlson asked him to assist his classes at the Brazilian Marines Head Quarters an offer promptly accepted by Oswaldo. From these classes the pair would fortify their friendship.<br />

Following Helio Gracie&#8217;s advice Paqueta started training weights differently and less often. Helio believed that all that weigh training was giving him the explosive strength but was taking alot of his muscle stamina away. The tip worked and Paqueta&#8217;s Jiu Jitsu improved heaps and bounds. </p>
<p>Though Paqueta was dedicated to Jiu Jitsu, he never slacked on his studying. Having been raised in a poor family taught him that, and so he graduated as a pedagogue. When he married he stopped training for a while to concentrate on his pedagogue career, but his life without Jiu Jitsu didn&#8217;t last for long as he started teaching Jiu Jitsu and Judo at the FUNABEN (Fundacao Para a Infancia e Adolescencia), the same institution he worked on as a director for many years. </p>
<p>In the late 1960&#8242;s the Jiu Jitsu Federation was created in a way to legitimize the sport, with it came the Black Belts. Before the Federation was created, Instructors would bear the Blue Belt, that was the distinguishing mark. Paquetá was never a man to pay attention to those sorts BJJ of politics and never requested his own Black Belt certificate, but in 1973 as the sport grew he saw the need to request it and so went to his old friend Carlson and asked him what was needed to get that belt. Carlson didn&#8217;t think twice, having seen the friend compete and train for so many years he told Paquetá he didn&#8217;t need to do any test and awarded him the black belt.<br />

Around 1991 he started tapping some fights, he had always used the camera to tape events in which his friends competed but it was around this period that Carlson Gracie asked Paqueta if he could tape his students on a tournament called &#8220;Atlantico Sul&#8221; which was organized by Marcos Vinicius. The idea was to run over the mistakes made on the mat later. With time Paqueta started taping more and more events, first as a hobby and many years later it became a business for him. </p>
<p>Though Paqueta received his black belt in 1973 only in 1993 did he receive his black belt certificate from the Federation. That caused a weird embarrassment for the federation later on as one of Oswaldo&#8217;s black belt students was to get his seventh degree (Setimo Grau) while the old master was still a simple Black Belt. Because of this Robson Gracie came to Paqueta to fix his status to Red Belt. </p>
<p>Today Paquetá is retired from Jiu Jitsu and his service at FUNABEN but he is still valued as one of the wisest men in Jiu Jitsu and his insight is often requested on fighting debates both online and on TV. His 4500 plus fight videos collection is regarded as the biggest private collection in Brazil.<br />
