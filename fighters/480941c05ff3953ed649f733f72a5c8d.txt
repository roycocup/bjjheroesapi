						</div><p><div id="attachment_770" class="wp-caption alignright" style="width: 200px"><a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/02/Sperry.jpg"><img src="http://www.bjjheroes.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/02/Sperry-190x300.jpg" alt="Mario Sperry" title="Sperry" width="190" height="300" class="size-medium wp-image-770" /></a><p class="wp-caption-text">Ze Mario Sperry</p></div>One of the main figures of Jiu Jitsu and Mixed Martial Arts (MMA) in the late 1990&#8242;s and early 2000&#8242;s, Ze Mario Sperry was at one point one of the main figures in Pride Fc in Japan and one of the leaders of Brazilian Top Team. He was also involved in one of the major team break ups in BJJ history between <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/carlson-gracie-profile">Carlson Gracie</a> and his students.<br />
.<br />
<strong>Name: </strong>Jose Mario Sperry<br />
.<br />
<strong>Nickname:</strong> He is often called &#8220;Zé&#8221; Mario, Zé is short for José in Portuguese.</p>
<p><strong>Ze Mario Sperry&#8217;s Lineage:</strong> Mitsuyo Maeda > <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/carlos-gracie-sr-profile">Carlos Gracie</a> > <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/carlson-gracie-profile">Carlson Gracie</a> > Ze Mario Sperry<br />
.<br />
<strong>Sperry&#8217;s Favorite Technique:</strong> Guard Pass and Clock Choke.<br />
.<br />
<strong>Jose Mario Sperry&#8217;s Weight Category (in BJJ)</strong> was Super Heavy Weight<br />
.<br />
<strong>José Mario Sperry&#8217;s Most Famous Fight (in BJJ)</strong> was against &#8220;<a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/roberto-gordo-correa">Gordo</a>&#8221; from Gracie-Barra at the final of the Mundial 1997, and his fight against <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/royce-gracie">Royler Gracie</a> (in which he tapped the Gracie which had never been tapped before &#8211; See Video at bottom of the page).<br />
.<br />
<strong>Sperry&#8217;s Main Achievements in (BJJ/Grappling/MMA):</strong> 3x World BJJ Champion (1996 Super Super Heavyweight, 1997 Super Heavyweight, 1998 Open Weight); 4x ADCC World Champion (1998 in both his category and the Open Weight, 1999 Superfight, 2000 Superfight).<br />
.<br />
<strong>Ze Mario Sperry&#8217;s Affiliation:</strong> Ze Mario started with Carlson Gracie and after the split we defended BTT&#8217;s colours (Brazilian Top Team).</p>
<h2>Jose Mario Sperry Biography</h2>
<p>Jose Mario Sperry was born on the 28th of September 1966, in Porto Alegre, Brazil. Like many other Jiu Jitsu practitioners from his generation, Sperry&#8217;s first approach to grappling was through Judo. he started Judo by the hands of Jorge Medhi, he only picked up Jiu Jitsu when he was a brown belt in Judo, he loved Jiu Jitsu so much he made it his life style, and within a month he received his Blue Belt from the hands of <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/carlson-gracie-profile">Master Carlson Gracie</a>, the same person that would award him with the Black Belt years later.</p>
<p>In Jorge Medhi&#8217;s gym (his Judo coach), many athletes prepared for the Olympics and Sperry trained with them every day, gaining alot of the physical attributes of an Olympic level athlete. Coming from a Military College, physical training was a big part of his day to day routine, having trained extensively in Water Polo, Volleyball Football (soccer) and Hammer Throw.</p>
<p>Sperry also has a degree in Economy and worked as an economist for a short period. That came to a hold when he was offered a job as a BJJ instructor for an academy in Porto Alegre in 1993. His first No-Holds-Barred fight was in 1995 in Niteroi, Brazil in a show called &#8220;Duelo de Titas&#8221;, his opponent was a fighter by the name of Tyson.<br />
Sperry was an amazing competitor in both Brazilian Jiu Jitsu and Submission Wrestling winning Gold at all the major tournaments in both sports, also venturing in &#8220;Vale-Tudo&#8221; the base of what Mixed Martial Arts are today. Always bringing the Carlson Gracie banner with him. In 2000 however, Sperry, <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/murilo-bustamante">Murilo Bustamante</a>, <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/ricardo-liborio">Ricardo Liborio</a> and &#8220;Bebeo&#8221; Duarte decided to leave the famous &#8220;Arrebentacao&#8221; team to make their own, ending the best Brazilian Jiu Jitsu team of that era, Carlson Gracie Team, but  creating one of the most successful MMA teams in the early 2000&#8242;s, Brazilian Top Team (BTT).</p>
<p>The big reason behind this was the fact that Carlson Gracie had moved to the United States, abd fighters like Sperry, Arona who competed in MMA and wanted to train with their master would have to travel to the US (all costs at the fighters own expense). Bustamante and Sperry had the idea of having coaches in Rio to do all aspects of the fight, Wrestling and Kickboxing coaches (leaving BJJ to them) to which Carlson disapproved as he wanted to supervise their training. </p>
<p>Sperry was also the manager of many important fighters such as the Nogueira Brothers (Rogerio and Rodrigo), Ricardo Arona and many others. Today Ze Mario Sperry is a entrepreneur and also has a part ownership in the &#8220;Jiu Jitsu Camp&#8221; a Brazilian project with foreign students in mind.</p>
