						</div><p><div id="attachment_916" class="wp-caption alignright" style="width: 249px"><a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/rafael-lovato-jr/attachment/lovato-jr" rel="attachment wp-att-916"><img src="http://www.bjjheroes.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/lovato-jr.jpg" alt="" title="lovato-jr" width="239" height="298" class="size-full wp-image-916" /></a><p class="wp-caption-text">Rafael Lovato</p></div>Rafael Lovato Junior (Jr.) is a man that made history in Brazilian Jiu Jitsu by being the first American to win the Brazilian National Championship (also known as the &#8220;Brasileiro&#8221;) in the black belt division. He was also in the first American family of black belts (himself and his father, Rafael Lovato senior), and on top of this he is also a World Champion and one of the toughest competitors in his division today.<br />
.</p>
<h2>Rafael Lovato Jr in Detail</h2>
<p><strong>Full Name:</strong> Rafael Lovato Junior<br />
.<br />
<strong>Lineage:</strong> Mitsuyo Maeda &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/carlos-gracie-sr-profile">Carlos Gracie Sr.</a> &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/carlos-carlinhos-gracie-jr">Carlos Gracie Junior</a> &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/carlos-machado">Carlos Machado</a> &gt; Rafael Lovato Jr<br />
.<br />
<strong>Main Titles in Jiu Jitsu:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>World Champion (2007);</li>
<li>World NoGi Champion (2010);</li>
<li>Brazilian National Champion (2007);</li>
<li>Pan American Champion (2008, 2007);</li>
<li>European Champion (2007);</li>
<li>Professional Submission League X-Mission Champion (2006);</li>
<li>Grappler&#8217;s Quest West Advanced No-Gi Champion (2005);</li>
<li>ADCC World Submission Wrestling Championships Quarterfinalist (2007)</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Weight Division:</strong> Pesado (Heavyweight) 94kg-207lbs<br />
.<br />
<strong>Favourite Position/Technique:</strong> Open guard, Triangle and Lapel Chokes.<br />
.<br />
<strong>Team/Association:</strong> Gracie Humaitá/University of Jiu Jitsu</p>
<h3>Rafael Lovato Jr Biography</h3>
<p>Rafael Lovato was born on the 25th of June of 1983  on Cincinnati, Ohio, United States of America, but he moved to Oklahoma City when he was 8 years of age.</p>
<p>Rafael&#8217;s father was an incredible Martial Arts buff, making his contact with combat sports inevitable. Still has a child Lovato got involved with boxing (his first true love by his own admission) and later with Jeet Kwon Do. When Rafael was 13 started his tuition in Brazilian Jiu Jitsu, again, through his father who was taking classes in Dallas from Carlos Machado. Lovato senior would travell to the Texan city once a week to have private classes with the Machado and then practice what he learned with his son.</p>
<p>When Rafael got to brown belt, he was already considered one of the toughest American competitors in the BJJ scene, and a hot prospect for the future. Lovato&#8217;s relationship with <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/saulo-ribeiro-facts-and-bio">Saulo Ribeiro</a> started around that time, when Rafael was 19 years of age. It was 2003 and Rafael had gotten to the final of the Arnold Invitational against Saulo Ribeiro, the two fought and in the end of the fight Saulo invited Rafael to come and train with him in Brazil which happened later that year when he visited the South American country on vacation. There the relationship between the two turned into a friendship and Lovato saw the potential of having a coach like Saulo beside him.</p>
<p>In the beggining of 2004 Lovato was invited to help Marc Laimon prepare for his fight with Ryron Gracie. The two spent 3 months as roomates in that training camp, creating a strong friendship that still stands today. After that camp  Rafael received his black belt in Jiu Jitsu on August 2004, just after his 21st birthday.<br />
In 2005 Rafael moved for good to the Ribeiro brothers academy (Saulo and <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/xande-ribeiro-fighter-wiki">Alexandre</a>) and made the most out of the new coaching, reaching the podium of most major tournaments he set foot on. And in 2007 he achieved the greatest glory of them all, winning the World Championship. Today he is still a very active fighter and a top competitor in any division he decides to compete in.</p>
