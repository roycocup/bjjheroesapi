<p>Ary Farias is probably the most exciting prospect in Jiu Jitsu&#8217;s Featherweight Division in recent years, the fighter has trained under some of the best teams in the World, such as ASLE, Checkmat and now Atos and will be a force to recon with in BJJ tournaments to come.</p>
<h2>Ary Farias in Detail</h2>
<p><strong>Nickname:</strong> &#8220;Aryzinho&#8221; is a gentle way of saying Ary in Portuguese.<br />
.<br />
<strong>Lineage:</strong> Mitsuyo Maeda &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/carlos-gracie-sr-profile">Carlos Gracie </a>&gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/carlson-gracie-profile">Carlson Gracie</a> &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/andre-pederneiras-bjj-and-mma-coach">Andre Pederneiras</a> &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/ramon-lemos-atos">Ramon Lemos</a> &gt; Ary Farias<br />
.<br />
<strong>Favourite Technique:</strong> Sweeping from Half Guard and Cross Choke from the Back.<br />
.<br />
<strong>Weight Category (BJJ):</strong> Featherweight (70kg &#8211; 154lbs) and Superfeatherweight (64kg &#8211; 141lbs).<br />
.<br />
<strong>Main Achievements (BJJ/Submission):</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>2x European Champion (Purple and Brown Belt)</li>
<li>8x World Champion (in the under 16s, Blue, Purple and Brown Belt Divisions)</li>
<li>Brazilian National Champion</li>
<li>World No-Gi Champion (Purple Belt Division).</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Affiliation/Team:</strong> Atos (Formerly Checkmat and ASLE).<br />
.</p>
<h3>Ary Farias Biography</h3>
<p>Ary Farias was born in Manaus, Brazil. He started training Jiu Jitsu at the famous ASLE academy when he was 11 years old. He had always been a big sports enthusiast, and fighting sports in particular, so it was a small step until he picked up on BJJ.</p>
<p>His first BJJ instructor was none other than World Champion <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/ronaldo-souza-jacare-fighter-wiki">Ronaldo “Jacare”</a>, who taught the kid’s classes at ASLE. Ary stayed with Jacare until he was 16, training in the champ every day, even sharing a house with him in the ASLE training camp. By the time Ary got to 16 years of age, he was already a huge prospect in BJJ winning every major tournament he got involved in. As he began getting involved with the adults classes in ASLE, which were taught by Master <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/henrique-machado-wiki-bio">Henrique Machado</a> (Jacare’s Coach), Ary had an argument with Mr Machado and decided to leave the Academy. He had met <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/alan-finfou-do-nascimento-wiki">Alain “Finfou” Nascimento</a> in one of the many tournaments he competed in, in Rio de Janeiro, and asked Alain if he could train with him at his gym, the Academia Fight Zone, where Alain trained under Ricardo Vieira’s guidance. &#8220;Finfou&#8221; intervened on Ary’s behalf and Ricardo Vieira accepted him in his gym, which involved a move to Rio de Janeiro.</p>
<p>With his parent’s support Ary moved to Rio de Janeiro, and started dedicating solely on his BJJ career, training his strength and conditioning in the famous X-Gym with Rogerio Camões and his Jiu Jitsu with <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/ricardo-ricardinho-vieira">Ricardinho Vieira</a> at Fightzone in Copacabana, the headquarters of Checkmat in Rio. This union was extremely fruitful and Ary Farias started to be regarded not only as a regional talent, but as a world wide prospect.</p>
<p>Ary received his Brown Belt from Ricardo Vieira in 2009, but on March 2010 decided to change camps and train with the Rio Claro team, Atos. Later that year (in November 2010) Ary Farias received his black belt from <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/ramon-lemos-atos">Ramon Lemos</a> in a cerimony celebrated in Rio Claro.</p>
