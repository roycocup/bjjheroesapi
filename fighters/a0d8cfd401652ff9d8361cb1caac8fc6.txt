								<div style="padding-top:0px;padding-right:0px;padding-bottom:0px;padding-left:0px;;">
							<iframe

						</div><div id="attachment_480" class="wp-caption alignright" style="width: 210px"><a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/01/arona.jpg"><img class="size-full wp-image-480" title="arona" src="http://www.bjjheroes.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/01/arona.jpg" alt="Arona" width="200" height="240" /></a><p class="wp-caption-text">Ricardo Arona</p></div>
<p>Ricardo Arona is a Brazilian Jiu Jitsu black belt and a Submission Wrestling legend, having won the ADCC (the World&#8217;s biggest Grappling Tournament) on 3 ocasions. He is also a successful Mixed Martial Artist (MMA fighter) that has fought some of the biggest names in the sport, fighters like Fedor Emilianenko, Wanderlei Silva, Mauricio &#8220;Shogun&#8221;, Murilo &#8220;Ninja&#8221;, amongst many others.<br />
.</p>
<h2>Ricardo Arona in Detail</h2>
<p><strong>Nickname:</strong> Brazilian Tiger was a nickname he received in the Pride FC days. This is due to his many Tiger Tattoo’s which he has throughout his body, and also because of Arona’s philosophy of fighting, were he says that in fighting you need to be the Tiger (predator) and never fear your prey. This chain of thought first came out when he gave an interview to Tatame Magazine.<br />
<strong> </strong></p>
<p><strong>Lineage:</strong> Mitsuyo Maeda &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/carlos-gracie-sr-profile">Carlos Gracie</a> &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/helio-gracie">Helio Gracie</a> &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/carlson-gracie-profile">Carlson Gracie</a> &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/ricardo-liborio">Ricardo Libório</a> &gt; Ricardo Arona.</p>
<p><strong>Main Achievements (BJJ/Grappling): </strong></p>
<ul>
<li>3x ADCC champion (2000, 2001 –  weight and absolute)</li>
<li>2x  World champion in Purple and Brown Belt</li>
<li>2nd  Place in Black Belt Division</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Favorite Technique: </strong>“Baiana” – Double leg takedown in Portuguese.</p>
<p><strong>Weight Category (BJJ):</strong> Peso Pesado (Heavyweight).</p>
<p><strong>Affiliation/Team:</strong> Brazilian Top Team (was Carlson Gracie in the beginning of his career).</p>
<h3>Ricardo Arona Biography</h3>
<p>Born on July 17, 1978 in Niteroi, Brazil.</p>
<p>It all changed in 1991 for Arona when he saw the Vale Tudo (No holds Barred) challenge between BJJ and Luta Livre (Brazilian form of Wrestling). The Brazilian Jiu Jitsu fighters cleaned the house and little Ricardo Arona thought “Damn, I want to do that too!” He had trained Karate and was in Judo, but dropped it straight away to start BJJ. His first experience wasn´t good, he didn´t like the teacher and after two months he changed academies.</p>
<p>After two years, with a proud blue belt around his waist, he changed again to Master Lélio&#8217;s place in a rudimentary class room at Lélio´s garage, Arona was 15 years old and all other students were adults.</p>
<p>Masters Lelio focused a lot on no-gi and enjoyed wrestling. Something that they trained a lot together with MMA. Training in this academy Ricardo Arona was crowned Brazilian Champion by 1994, after which he received his Purple Belt.</p>
<p>By then, Master Lelio was very much aware of what huge talent Arona had, and that he wouldn´t progress in his small gym. He advised Arona to pursue his training elsewhere, and the first door he knocked was Nova União. Unfortunately he wasn´t well accepted there and he decided to move on to Carlson Gracie´s Academy.</p>
<p>Arona came to Carlson´s academy wearing his purple belt and he was 18 years old. His real ambition was to fight MMA but he was told that he was too young to start MMA as there were plenty of Black Belts at the club representing the team. Hierarchy was important in Carlson Gracie´s “Arrebentação” gym and Arona understood that, so he went back to training with the kimono.</p>
<p>During that year he won the Rio de Janeiro state championship and the Mundial (world Championship) getting his Brown Belt on the following year. The year he won the brown belt he went to win the Rio de Janeiro State Championship again and the Brazilian Nationals as well as the Mundial (also again) in his weight class, finishing second in the absolute division. Still that year he won the Brazilian ADCC trials (the biggest Submission Wrestling Championship in the world, being Brazil the hardest trials to win).</p>
<p>Next came his biggest achievement, ADCC which brought him worldwide recognition. After winning the Championship in Abu Dhabi he received his black belt from the hands of Ricardo Liborio (while still on the Podium). After the big win, promotions started dropping contracts on his lap to go and fight, which was Ricardo´s intent all along.</p>
<p>It was hard for him to find time to put on a gi and train, as Submission Wrestling and Mixed Martial Arts were his main focus now. Still he went on to compete at the World Championship, training only 12 days with the gi previous to the event. He finished second winning the Silver Medal in his weight class.</p>
<p>A few years later he claimed that with time BJJ became a passion and that if it was monetarily viable he would have followed a career with the Kimono.</p>
<p>Before he had joined the Pride FC Organization, he fought in RINGS winning the belt in what was one of the most prestigious organizations in the beginning of the century.</p>
<p>Arona´s conditioning has never been in doubt, always turning up in shape for his fights. He dedicated a lot of time developing ways to improve his strength and conditioning. For this, he says he likes to train with nature, having developed a set of exercises from walking against sea´s current to develop balance, running up hills on a beach for stamina and doing extreme manoeuvres while surfing in the beach of Itacoatira to test his adrenaline dump.</p>
<p>Arona went on fight numerous times in Pride FC, one of the most prestigious organizations in the World (now extinct), after which he stepped back to relax. Rumour is that he is returning in 2010, possibly to the UFC, we at BJJ Heroes will be rooting for the Brazilian Tiger.</p>
