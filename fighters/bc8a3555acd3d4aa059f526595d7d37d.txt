						</div><p><div id="attachment_329" class="wp-caption alignright" style="width: 210px"><a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/01/Rickson.jpg"><img src="http://www.bjjheroes.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/01/Rickson.jpg" alt="Rickson Gracie" title="Rickson" width="200" height="240" class="size-full wp-image-329" /></a><p class="wp-caption-text">Rickson Gracie</p></div>Rickson Gracie is a Martial Arts legend and one of the pioneers in the sport of mixed martial arts (MMA). Today he is a teacher of Brazilian Jiu Jitsu and one of the most recognized figures of the sport.<br />
.<br />
<strong>Rickson Gracie&#8217;s Lineage:</strong> Mitsuyo Maeda > <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/carlos-gracie-sr-profile">Carlos Gracie</a> > <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/helio-gracie">Helio Gracie</a> > Rickson Gracie<br />
.<br />
<strong>Rickson Gracie’s Favorite Technique:</strong> Mata Leao (RNC) choke<br />
.<br />
<strong>Rickson Gracie’s Weight:</strong> He competed at 85Kg.<br />
.<br />
<strong>Rickson Gracie’s most Famous Fight </strong>was against Rei Zulu in Rio, Brazil 1984, which he won by choke.<br />
.<br />
<strong>Rickson Gracie’s main Achievements (BJJ/ MMA/ Grappling):</strong> Rickson was actively competing in sport BJJ when there was no Mundial (World Championships) organization to compete in. He did compete in smaller competitions everywhere he could at the time, having never been defeated in sport BJJ.</p>
<p>He is also the holder of an 11-0 Professional MMA record.</p>
<p>Common myth is that Rickson’s record is of 400-0.That is including all competitions (BJJ, Judo, Sambo and MMA and closed doors No-Holds-Barred fights  &#8211; common of the early 80’s Jiu Jitsu era in Brazil) in which he was involved. However that record can be disputed since there is at least one loss recorded in sport Sambo (by points – throw) against Ron Tripp.<br />
.<br />
<strong>Rickson’s Association/Team:</strong> Gracie Jiu Jitsu</p>
<h2>Rickson Gracie&#8217;s Biography</h2>
<p>Rickson Gracie was born in November 20, 1958 in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. He is the son of Jiu Jitsu Grandmaster <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/helio-gracie">Helio Gracie</a> and considered by many the greatest Gracie Jiu Jitsu practitioner in the family’s history, and having been praised by many BJJ champions (like Paulo Filho, <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/demian-maia-fighter-wiki">Demian Maia</a> and <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/ricardo-aronas-fighter-profile">Ricardo Arona</a>) as the best BJJer they haver rolled with.</p>
<p>Rickson’s youth was spent on the mats, he was raised to be the next family representative in the fighting World, following his predecessors path&#8217;s, people like his father (the first representative of the Gracie Clan), his cousin <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/carlson-gracie-profile">Carlson</a> (the second family representative) and his other cousin <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/rolls-gracie-profile">Rolls</a>. Always considering himself of being a Gracie Jiu Jitsu practitioner and not an MMA fighter, he took pride on the fact that he did not cross train in other martial arts in order to compete against the best.</p>
<p>In 1980 <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/waldemar-santana-profile">Waldemar Santana</a>, the man who had defeated Rickson’s father &#8211;  Helio Gracie &#8211; on an historic no holds barred event, and with whom he later regained contact with, asked if the Gracie&#8217;s had anyone in their class that could challenge Rei Zulu (Santana’s student). Zulu was a huge fighter with incredible athletic ability and was undefeated in his NHB record. No one else wanted to fight him in Brazil and Waldemar who was Zulu’s promoter and instructor was finding it very hard to find matches for his fighter.</p>
<p>Rolls Gracie, the family’s star stud jumped on the occasion of fighting Zulu, but Helio had other plans, Rickson Gracie. Rickson was 18 years old at the time and weighed around 20kg less then Zulu, but he accepted his dad’s decision and fought. The fight was in Brasilia under Rei Zulu’s home crowd and Rickson didn’t feel 100%. Carlos Gracie, a firm believer in spiritualism and Aura, asked Rickson not to fight as he didn’t feel this was his fight. But still he went along with it. </p>
<p>The fight was long and hard, but Rickson finished it with a Mata Leao (RNC). Rickson later said he felt dizzy the whole fight, dizziness which was attributed to the change of air in Brasilia (much dryer then the Rio de Janeiro air he was used to).</p>
<p>Rickson second fight was again against Rei Zulu (Zulu King), this time in Maracanãzinho, a venue that held 40,000 seats and was packed at the time of the fight. There had been a bit of smack talk from Zulu’s camp and the Gracies arranged a second fight, this time with the added pressure of a large venue. The battle was tremendous and again Rickson was victorious by choke.</p>
<p>Rickson then ventured to Japan to promote the family’s legacy on the Land of the Rising Sun. He defeated several fighters always by submission.</p>
<p>Rickson’s last fight was meant to be against the man who defeated most of his brothers and cousins, Kasushi Sakuraba, also known as “The Gracie Hunter”. Rickson agreed to fight Sakuraba but had to pull out due to his son’s (Rockson) sudden death (officially by motorcycle accident, although there are rumours that his ending might have been more sinister then it appeared to be at first). Rickson was devastated after this and stopped training all together for an extended period. He never competed again, but he restarted training and teaching, especially to carry on the family tradition and make his other son, Kron a Gracie super star.</p>
