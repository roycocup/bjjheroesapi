<p>Ricardo Abreu (Also known as Ricardo Demente in BJJ) is a top Jiu Jitsu Black Belt competitor from the Nova Uniao BJJ team. After spending 3 years out of Jiu Jitsu due to lack of sponsorship, he returned in 2009 with great wins over some of BJJ&#8217;s very best, he has maintained his good form, having been invited as a grappling sparring partners by many big names in MMA (mixed martial arts) such as UFC standout Anderson “the spider” Silva.<br />
.</p>
<h2>Ricardo Abreu in Detail</h2>
<p><strong>Nickname:</strong> Demente in Portuguese stands for &#8220;Madman&#8221;, the reason his training partners gave him the nickname was because when Ricardo started training, he would often &#8220;give&#8221; his arm while in someone&#8217;s guard when rolling in order to pass. People would often stretch his arm to the limit, but he would more then often prevail and pass. For this his coach and colleagues started calling him Madman (Demente).<br />
.<br />
<strong>Lineage:</strong> Mitsuyo Maeda &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/carlos-gracie-sr-profile">Carlos Gracie </a>&gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/carlson-gracie-profile">Carlson Gracie</a> &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/andre-pederneiras-bjj-and-mma-coach">Andre Pederneiras</a> &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/robson-moura-bjj-fighter">Robson Moura</a> &gt; Ricardo Abreu<br />
.<br />
<strong>Favourite Technique/Position:</strong> Sweep from butterfly guard and Armbar from side control<br />
.<br />
<strong>Weight Category:</strong> Super Pesado (Super Heavy weight)<br />
.<br />
<strong>Main Achievements (BJJ/Grappling):</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>2x Silver Medallist World Championships (Black Belt 2010, 2009)</li>
<li>European Champion (2010)</li>
<li>Sao Paulo State Champion (Black Belt &#8211; Weight &amp; Open Weight 2009)</li>
<li>Open Weight South American Champion (CBJJE 2009)</li>
<li>2x World Pro Cup South Brazilian Trials Winner (2009, 2010)</li>
<li>Bronze Medalist in World Cup &#8211; CBJJO (Brown Belt 2004)</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Team/Association:</strong> Nova Uniao &#8211; Ribeirao Preto</p>
<h2>Ricardo &#8220;Demente&#8221; Abreu Biography</h2>
<p>Ricardo Abreu was born in Cidade de Dourados (Mato Grosso) but came to live in Ribeirao Preto in the State of Sao Paulo, Brazil, when he was 9 years of age, he began training Jiu Jitsu when he was 14 years old.</p>
<p>Training out of &#8220;Academia Winner&#8221;, he was one of the hottest prospects in Nova Uniao BJJ Team, who has always lacked a serious contender in the Heavy Weight categories. Winning the Brazilian National championships 5x in the lower belt divisions, he was considered <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/andre-galvao-bjj-and-mma-fighter-wiki">Andre Galvao&#8217;s</a> nemesis on the Brown Belt, as Galvao was unbeatable in that division until he faced Ricardo, who on two occasions defeated the <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/fernando-augusto-terere-bio">Terere</a> protégé (on the World Cup 2004 and on FESP Championship, this time as a Black Belt in 2005).</p>
<p>Although the future seemed to be bright ahead, Ricardo decided to pull out of Jiu Jitsu and stopped training completely in 2006. The reason was none other then lack of the always important sponsorships, the problem that haunts so many Jiu Jitsu fighters throughout the world. During the period in which &#8220;Demente&#8221; was off the mats, he dedicated himself to his academic career in law, he worked as an consultation specialist in a finance firm until 3 years later, an unexpected proposal from a sponsor came across and Ricardo Took it with both hands, with just 5 months to prepare himself, he finished 2nd in his weight class at the world Championships in 2009, submitting the former champion on the way to the podium.</p>
<p>His good streak of results continued throughout 2009, winning the Sao Paulo State Championships (one of the toughest in Brazil) submitting every single adversary in both his weight class and the open weight division, but an injury on his shoulder prevented him from fighting in the World Pro Cup Final in Abu Dhabi, the biggest prize money competition in the World of Jiu Jitsu.</p>
<p>2010 also started with an injury, this time in his knee, which needed surgery, but a week after Ricardo Abreu was back into training and although he was feeling just up to 50% of his abilities, he still managed to win the Europeans in the end of January.</p>
