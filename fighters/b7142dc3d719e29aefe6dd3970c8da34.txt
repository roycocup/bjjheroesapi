Today although semi-retired from competition, Leo is still considered one of the “<a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-news/top-10-bjj-fighters-of-all-time">All Time Great’s</a>” of Jiu Jitsu.<br />
.</p>
<h2>Leo Vieira in Detail</h2>
<p><strong>Nickname:</strong> “Leozinho” which means little Leonardo<br />
.<br />
<strong>Lineage:</strong> Mitsuyo Maeda &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/carlos-gracie-sr-profile">Carlos Gracie</a> &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/helio-gracie">Helio Gracie</a> &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/rolls-gracie-profile">Rolls Gracie</a> &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/romero-jacare-cavalcanti-wiki">Romero Cavalcanti</a> Leonardo Vieira<br />
.<br />
<strong>Favourite Position/Technique:</strong> Leo was always famous for his athletic and spectacular guard passes.<br />
.<br />
<strong>Main Achievements in BJJ/ Grappling:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>2x ADCC Champion (2003, 2005)</li>
<li>2x World Jiu Jitsu Champion (1996 – brown belt, 1998 Black Belt)</li>
<li>2x Pan American Champion (2002, 2004)</li>
</ul>
<p>.<br />
<strong>Weight Division in BJJ:</strong> “Peso Leve” – Lightweight (76Kg)<br />
.<br />
<strong>Association/Team:</strong> Checkmat (Previously associated with Brasa, Master Team and Alliance).<br />
.</p>
<h3>Leozinho Vieira Biography</h3>
<p>Leonardo Santos was born on the 23rd of March 1976, in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. He is the eldest of 3 brothers, all of them accomplished BJJ Black Belts (<a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/ricardo-ricardinho-vieira">Ricardo Vieira</a> and Leandro Vieira). Leonardo or “Leo” as he is called amongst the Jiu Jitsu community started training when he was just 6 years of age at the “Academia Romero Jacare” with the legendary BJJ coach <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/romero-jacare-cavalcanti-wiki">Romero Cavalcanti</a>.</p>
<p>In 1993 his Master Cavalcanti merged the &#8220;Romero Jacare&#8221; team with some of his former students and formed “Team Alliance”, Leo competed for this new team for many years, receiving his Black Belt under the Alliance banner when he was 21 years old, by the hands of his master, after winning the historic first World Championships event ever as a Brown Belt (1996), in a final fought against Mauricio Mariano from Gracie Barra.</p>
<p>The following year, his first as a Black Belt, Leo fought his way to the final against Marcio Feitosa in the first of many historical duels between the two. This first fight went to the Gracie Barra fighter, Feitosa, but in 1998 when the two met once again in the final, Leonardo was the victor in one of the hardest fights of the event. 1998 was the last time Leonardo won the World Championship, although he did make the final again in 1999 (losing to Royler Gracie) and placed 3rd in 2000.</p>
<p>His Career’s highest point had not been reached however, that came in 2003 as Leonardo won the World’s most prestigious Submission Grappling medal, the ADCC (Abu Dhabi Combat Club), a gold medal achieved again in 2005.</p>
<p>In between this period, Leozinho had moved to Sao Paulo and was training under <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/fabio-gurgel-wiki">Fabio Gurgel</a> at the Team Alliance HQ there, but in 2002 there was a small rebellion against the head coach Gurgel and Leonardo left the team together with most of the black belts that trained there. The argument was over the “Mundial” (World Championships) which occurred a week after the “Copa do Mundo” (World Cup). As the “Copa do Mundo” offered a good prize money to its competitors and the “Mundial” didn’t, Leonardo and his team mates felt they should support this organization (CBJJO – Confederação de Jiu Jitsu Olimpico) and compete in it, Gurgel however decided against it, as he felt that competing in the Cup was less prestigious then competing in the World Championship. The fighters went on to fight at the “Copa” and Gurgel banned them from the gym, and so Leozinho and the others formed another team, “Master Team” after a few problems with that team also, another team was formed, which was called “Brasa” (which is slang for “Brazil” but also means “Ember”).</p>
<p>The Brasa Team went on for years and still exists today, but in 2008 Leo and his brothers decided to open another team in which they had more autonomy, this team they called Checkmat.</p>
<p>Today, Leonardo is one of the headcoaches at the Checkmat academy in Sao Paulo.</p>
