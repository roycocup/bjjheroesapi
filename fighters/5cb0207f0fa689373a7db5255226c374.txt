										</div><p><div id="attachment_1085" class="wp-caption alignright" style="width: 210px"><a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/chico-mendes/attachment/chico-mendes-fireball" rel="attachment wp-att-1085"><img src="http://www.bjjheroes.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/Chico-Mendes-Fireball-200x300.jpg" alt="" title="Chico-Mendes-Fireball" width="200" height="300" class="size-medium wp-image-1085" /></a><p class="wp-caption-text">Chico Mendes</p></div>Chico Mendes is a Brazilian Jiu Jitsu black belt under the legendary <a title="Leonardo Vieira" href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/leo-vieira-facts-and-bio">Leo Vieira</a> an honour he received in 2004. <h2>Chico Mendes in Detail</h2>
<p><strong>Full Name:</strong> Francisco Ramos Mendes Neto</p>
<p><strong>Nickname:</strong> “Chico” which is short for Francisco in Portuguese.</p>
<p><strong>Lineage:</strong> Mitsuyo Maeda &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/carlos-gracie-sr-profile">Carlos Gracie</a> &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/helio-gracie">Helio Gracie</a> &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/rolls-gracie-profile">Rolls Gracie</a> &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/romero-jacare-cavalcanti-wiki">Romero Cavalcanti</a> &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/leo-vieira-facts-and-bio">Leonardo Vieira</a> &gt; Chico Mendes</p>
<p><strong>Main Achievements:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>World Cup Champion (2010);</li>
<li>BJJ Pro Cup Silver Medallist (2011);</li>
<li>International Master &amp; Senior Silver Medallist (2010 – master);</li>
<li>Brazilian National Bronze Medallist (2010 – master);</li>
<li>4th place – State Circuit of Rio de Janeiro Final Ranking (2005);</li>
<li>Scandinavian Open Champion 2005 (absolute and division);</li>
<li>Brazilian National Teams Silver Medallist (2004);</li>
<li>Brazilian National Champion (2004 brown);</li>
<li>São Paulo University Games 3rd place (2004 – nogi);</li>
<li>State Circuit of Rio de Janeiro Final Ranking Champion (2003);</li>
<li>Brazilian National Teams Cup 3rd place (2003)</li>
<li>2x BJJ Pro Cup Bronze Medallist (2011 weight &#038; absolute);</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Weight Division:</strong> Peso Médio (82kg-181lbs)</p>
<p><strong>Favourite Position:</strong> Half-Guard from the top.</p>
<p><strong>Team/Association:</strong> Checkmat</p>
<h3>Chico Mendes Biography</h3>
<p>Francisco “Chico” Mendes was born 14th of July, 1980 in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. He started training Jiu Jitsu in his early teens at <a title="Jacare Cavalcanti" href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/romero-jacare-cavalcanti-wiki">Romero “Jacaré” Cavalcanti</a> academy, though his introduction class was given by <a title="Leozinho Vieira" href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/leo-vieira-facts-and-bio">Leonardo Vieira</a> (who was a purple belt at the time). At the time the gym had a huge range of talented people giving classes, such as: Jamelão, Jacaré, <a title="Ricardo Miller" href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/ricardo-franjinha-miller">Franjinha</a> and of course, Leozinho Vieira.</p>
<p>Chico Mendes received all his yellow (in 1994) and blue belts from the Alliance master Romero Cavalcanti and Leo Vieira, and after master Jacaré went to the United States and Leozinho to Sao Paulo, Mendes moved his training to <a title="Otavio Ratinho" href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/octavio-couto-ratinho">Octavio Couto</a>’s gym (Also known as Ratinho). Training there he achieved both his purple and his brown belt. In 2003/2004 he moved to Sao Paulo, Brazil to be reunited with Leo Vieira.</p>
<p>In December 2004 Chico Mendes was awarded his black belt by Vieira. In the beginning of 2011 after some time travelling and displaying Checkmat’s jiu jitsu in seminars around the world, Chico settled in England, United Kingdom, teaching at Trojan Free Fighters in three different locations (Cheltenham, Bristol and Worcester).</p>
