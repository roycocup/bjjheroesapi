<p>One of the main American figures in Jiu Jitsu and Submission Wrestling tournaments since the turn of the millennium, the Hawaiian Baret Yoshida became a household name for ADCC fans around the world. An Egan Inoue black belt, Baret Yoshida fought against the elite of BJJ with some very solid wins – such as his win over <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/alexandre-soca-freitas">Alexandre Soca</a> (2001) – and his exquisite use of the guard quickly turned him into a fan favourite. Baret Yoshida also competed in MMA (Mixed Martial Arts) becoming an important grappling coach at the same time; he is also a published author with a <a href="http://gan.doubleclick.net/gan_click?lid=41000000034850370&#038;pubid=21000000000302733">grappling book</a> and <a href="http://gan.doubleclick.net/gan_click?lid=41000000034850370&#038;pubid=21000000000302733">DVD</a> that were very well accepted by the BJJ media.</p>
<h2>Baret Yoshida in Detail</h2>
<p><strong>Lineage:</strong> Mitsuyo Maeda &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/carlos-gracie-sr-profile">Carlos Gracie </a> &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/carlson-gracie-profile">Carlson Gracie</a> &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/andre-pederneiras-bjj-and-mma-coach">Andre Pederneiras</a> &gt; John Lewis &gt; Egan Inoue &gt; Barret Yoshida</p>
<p><strong>Main Achievements/Record:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>2x World Nogi Champion (2008, 2009);</li>
<li>3x Copa Hawaii Champion (2004, 2005, 2006);</li>
<li>2x ADCC Silver Medallist (2003, 2001);</li>
<li>World Nogi Bronze Medallist (2010);</li>
<li>ADCC Bronze Medallist (2007);</li>
<li>2x Pan American Bronze Medallist (1998 blue, 1999 purple)</li>
<li>NAGA Superfight Champion (2000);</li>
<li>2x Advanced Gi Champion (2000 weight and absolute – NAGA);</li>
<li>Uemura Cup Champion (1996)</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Weight Division:</strong> Peso Pena (70kg-154lbs) and Pluma (64kg-141lbs)</p>
<p><strong>Favourite Position/Technique:</strong> Guard (Open)</p>
<p><strong>Team/Association:</strong> Undisputed</p>
<p><strong>Important DVD Releases:</strong> <a title="Baret Yoshida 3 DVD Set" href="http://gan.doubleclick.net/gan_click?lid=41000000035438231&#038;pubid=21000000000302733" target="_blank">No Gi Techniques</a></p>
<h3>Baret Yoshida’s Biography</h3>
<p>Baret Yoshida was born in Hawaii, United States on the 26th of April, 1975. He started training Jiu Jitsu in 1996 at Relson Gracie’s academy, one of the oldest and most established academies in the region. He trained under the Gracie for a number of years, achieving his purple belt with him. In 1999 however, he decided to switch camps and move to the Inoue brother’s gym, led by Egan and Enson &#8220;Yamato Damashii&#8221; Inoue.</p>
<p>Though Baret already had important wins at an international stage in the lower belts, it was his participation in the 1999 ADCC (Abu Dhabi Combat Club) that put Yoshida’s name on the map. Baret Yoshida had fought on the trials and made it to the final, though he lost. He was called to the finals as an alternate because one of the competitors had injured himself and could no longer compete. In that tournament, the younger and less experienced Baret gave a very tough challenge to one of the most dominant fighters to have ever competed, <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/royler-gracie-facts-and-bio">Royler Gracie</a>. Yoshida lost a chance to medal, but he gained a legion of fans, and was invited into the tournament in the following years. In those tournaments, Yoshida would solidify his name as one of the best Nogi competitors in the world medalling on 3 occasions in arguably the toughest submission wrestling tournaments in the world.</p>
<p>An admitted admirer of the Nogi game, Baret decided to also taste the feel of Mixed Martial Arts action. He has had somewhat of a mixed record, fighting mainly big names such as Jeff Curran and Masahiro Oishi.</p>
<p>After a long list of achievements, including a silver medal at the world’s most challenging Nogi competition, Egan Inoue awarded Baret Yoshida his black belt in BJJ, in 2002. With the prestige and recognition, Baret also released a <a href="http://gan.doubleclick.net/gan_click?lid=41000000034850972&#038;pubid=21000000000302733">series of DVD&#8217;s and book</a> that had tremendous acceptance by the Jiu Jitsu community.</p>
