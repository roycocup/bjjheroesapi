						</div><p><div id="attachment_771" class="wp-caption alignright" style="width: 250px"><a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/09/Josef-ze-cobra-manuel.jpg"><img src="http://www.bjjheroes.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/09/Josef-ze-cobra-manuel-240x300.jpg" alt="Ze Cobra" title="Josef-ze-cobra-manuel" width="240" height="300" class="size-medium wp-image-771" /></a><p class="wp-caption-text">Josef Manuel</p></div>Josef Manuel Junior, mostly known by his “war name” of “Zé Cobra”, is a martial artist with over 20 years experience in the fight game. He is also a Brazilian Jiu Jitsu black belt under one of the legends in the sport, the honourable <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/robson-moura-bjj-fighter">Robson Moura</a>.<br />
.<br />
<strong>Full Name:</strong> Josef Manuel Junior<br />
.<br />
<strong>Meaning of Nickname:</strong> “Zé Cobra” basically means “Joe Snake” a name frowned upon by Josef in the beginning of his career, but a name he embraced later on. The name comes parallel with Josef’s first steps in BJJ. As a teen he met a Brazilian family that accepted Josef as their own, the Macedo family. With them he learned all about the Brazilian culture and even how to read, write &#038; speak Portuguese. Josef’s first Jiu Jitsu instructor knew the Macedos very well and would rarely speak with Josef in English at the Academy. Josef would often study his instructor &#038; use his own moves against Him. The coach would say -sparring “Porra Ze voce é malandro pa ca****o, igual a uma Cobra” – “Damn Zé, you are getting sneaky, just like a snake!” And so he started being called, &#8220;Zé Cobra!&#8221;<br />
.<br />
<strong>Josef Manuel Lineage:</strong> Mitsuyo Maeda > <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/carlos-gracie-sr-profile">Carlos Gracie </a>> <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/carlson-gracie-profile">Carlson Gracie</a> > <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/andre-pederneiras-bjj-and-mma-coach">Andre Pederneiras</a> > <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/robson-moura-bjj-fighter">Robson Moura</a> > Josef Manuel<br />
.<br />
<strong>Josef Manuel’s Main Titles:</strong> No-Gi World Champion (2009 &#8211; Master); No-Gi Pan American Silver Medallist (2009); European Champion (2010 – Master); New York Open Silver Medallist (2010)<br />
.<br />
<strong>Josef Manuel’s Weight Category:</strong> Competed as a “Pena” &#8211; Featherweight in the beginning of his career, but changed to “Pluma” and even “Galo” in the latter years. “Ze Cobra” is also an avid competitor in the “Absoluto”.<br />
.<br />
<strong>Josef Manuel’s Team/Association:</strong> Nova Uniao (Robson Moura Nova Uniao – RMNU)</p>
<h2>Josef Manuel “Zé Cobra” Biography</h2>
<p>Josef Manuel Junior was born on the 12th of November 1974, in Newark, New Jersey (The Southside). He engaged in martial arts from a very early age, putting on his first pair of boxing gloves when he was around 4 or 5 years old. His first contact with Brazilian Jiu Jitsu was like thousands of others, by watching the very first Ultimate Fighting Championship, however, it wasn’t until a few years later that he got a chance to actually train BJJ.</p>
<p>It was in 2001 that Josef Manuel was introduced to Mário Lemos, a former student of the famous Gracie Academy in Humaitá where he had been instructed by two of the greatest legends in BJJ, <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/rickson-gracie-facts-and-bio">Rickson</a> and <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/royler-gracie-facts-and-bio">Royler Gracie</a>. By this time Josef had very strong ties to the Brazilian community in Newark, through the Macedo family, a family of Brazilian origin that had embraced Josef as their own. Professor Mario Lemos had a good relationship with the Macedos and also with Josef. As Josef started developing his BJJ game and excelling his old master, Mario Lemos would use a famous Rickson Gracie saying as a joke to Josef: &#8220;Você cria cobra depois você tem que viver com elas&#8221; meaning – “you raise snakes then you have to live with them”.</p>
<p>Always searching for the best instruction to evolve in Jiu Jitsu, in 2005 Josef “Ze Cobra” switched camps and tutor, to train with Robson Moura, a famous 5x World Champion in the black belt division and a legendary “pluma” from the Nova Uniao training camp. Almost 4 years later (on February 2009) Josef Manuel finally received the long awaited black belt under “Robinho”. Later that year Josef Manuel also had the honour of training soldiers from the United States Army. As his career grew in that department he was also offered the position to train the Federa; Agents and Local Law Enforcement Officials.</p>
