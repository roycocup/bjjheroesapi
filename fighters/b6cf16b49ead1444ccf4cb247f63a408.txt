<p>Ricardo Vieira also known as Ricardinho or Rico Vieira in the Jiu Jitsu world, is a 9x World BJJ Champion and one of the best coaches in the World, him together with his brother <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/leo-vieira-facts-and-bio">Leozinho Vieira</a> lead the famous Checkmat academy, Ricardinho in Rio de Janeiro at Fightzone and Leozinho in Sao Paulo.<br />
.</p>
<h2>Ricardo Vieira in Detail</h2>
<p><strong>Nicknames:</strong> Ricardinho and Rico &#8211; The suffix “inho” in Portuguese is usually used as a gentle and warm way of saying someone’s name. It can also mean little, like “Little Ricardo”. Rico is another way of saying Ricardo.<br />
.<br />
<strong>Lineage:</strong> Mitsuyo Maeda &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/carlos-gracie-sr-profile">Carlos Gracie</a> &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/helio-gracie">Helio Gracie</a> &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/rolls-gracie-profile">Rolls Gracie</a> &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/romero-jacare-cavalcanti-wiki">Romero Cavalcanti</a> &gt; Ricardo Vieira<br />
.<br />
<strong>Main Achievements (BJJ): </strong></p>
<ul>
<li> 6x World Champion (2001 Black, 2000 &amp; 1999 Brown, 1998 Purple, 1997 &amp; 1996 Blue)</li>
<li>Pan American Champion (2002 Black Belt)</li>
<li>World Cup Champion (CBJJO)</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Weight Category:</strong> Super Featherweight (64Kg – 141lbs).<br />
.<br />
<strong>Team/Association:</strong> Vieira Bros – Checkmat</p>
<h3>Ricardo Vieira Biography</h3>
<p>Ricardo Vieira was born on the 9th of January 1979 in Rio de Janeiro Brazil. He is the brother of Jiu Jitsu fighters <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/leo-vieira-facts-and-bio">Leonardo Vieira</a> and Leandro Vieira. Ricardo first stepped on a jiu jitsu mat at the age of 5 being coached by legendary <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/romero-jacare-cavalcanti-wiki">Romero Cavalcanti</a>. His aptitude to BJJ shinned right from the beginning with multiple trophies from a young age, including at the World stage, the Mundial a competition he won in every belt division he competed, from Blue Belt to Black Belt.</p>
<p>After two straight years in the podium of the “Mundial” as a Brown Belt (1998 and 1999), Ricardo was promoted by his instructor to Black Belt. Now in the major league he faced the most fierce Super Featherweight fighter in history, <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/robson-moura-bjj-fighter">Robson Moura</a>. This epic battle took place in 2001 World Championship&#8217;s semi finals, with the victory going to Ricardinho as he went on to win the title.</p>
<p>After a few years as a black belt with many more medals around his neck (including the World Cup – CBJJO) Ricardo Vieira slowly started disappearing from the Jiu Jitsu competitive scene, this happened mainly because he started coaching at his academy, assisting Mr Cavalcanti. After his master left to the USA (where he formed an academy in Atlanta), Ricardinho took the helm, with <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/rodrigo-comprido-medeiros">Rodrigo &#8220;Comprido&#8221; Medeiros</a> by his side ( &#8220;Comprido&#8221; also moved to the US later on, leaving Ricardinho to lead the Rio de Janeiro team by himself).</p>
<p>In 2002 Ricardo was one of the fighters who left Alliance Team (with most of the main fighters in the team tagging along) after an internal dispute, and formed VB Team together with his brothers (the V.B. stands for “Vieira Brothers”) the VB Team then associated with Brasa, another team that was formed off the split with Alliance. Ricardo worked with Brasa for the most of 4 years. After which he left to form Checkmat with his brothers.</p>
<p>Ricardo Vieira is also a synonym of charity, as he has took over Fernando Augusto’s project in the Cantagalo Slums in Rio de Janeiro. A project raised to take children off the streets and away from gun crime, a very common end for kids in this impoverished part of the city. The project is based on Jiu Jitsu, teaching those that cannot afford being taught for free while building their social skills and teaching them the benefits of citizenship.</p>
<p>Ricardo Vieira’s and VB Team’s saying is “Nunca desista dos seus sonhos” – Never give up your dreams, a testament that Ricardo abides by.</p>
<p>In 2009 Ricardo made reappearance to a BJJ competition in Stockholm, Sweden, winning the absolute division in the Stockholm BJJ Open.</p>
