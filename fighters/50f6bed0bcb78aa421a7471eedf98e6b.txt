<p>Roberto Godoi is a brilliant Jiu Jitsu instructor formed by Waldomiro Perez. The founder of one of the strongest teams in Sao Paulo, Brazil &#8211; The Macaco/Godoi (today Godoi Jiu JItsu), Godoi is also famed for his bitter break up with his team partner Jorge &#8220;Macaco&#8221; Patino in 2001, a break up that made the headlines of many BJJ magazines and turned into a fearce rivalry on and off the mats.</p>
<h2>Roberto Godoi in Detail</h2>
<p><strong>Lineage:</strong> Mitsuyo Maeda &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/carlos-gracie-sr-profile">Carlos Gracie</a> &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/helio-gracie">Helio Gracie</a> &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/rickson-gracie-facts-and-bio">Rickson Gracie</a> &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/marcelo-behring">Marcelo Behring</a> &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/waldomiro-perez-jr">Waldomiro Perez</a> &gt; Roberto Godoi</p>
<p><strong>Main Achievements/BJJ record:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Campeonato Mundial No-Gi Champion</strong> (2008 weight and open weight Masters Division);</li>
<li><strong>5x Brazilian National Champio</strong>n (2007 &#8211; Masters Division, 2009 weight and open weight Masters Division, 2010 weight and open weight Masters Division);</li>
<li><strong>3x Pan American Silver Medallist</strong> (2007 Masters Division, 2008 Masters Division, 2009 open weight Masters Division);</li>
<li><strong>4x Pan American Bronze Medallist</strong> (2000, 2001, 2002, 2004);</li>
<li><strong>Brazilian National Silver Medallist</strong> (2000)</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Weight Category:</strong> Pesado (94kg-207lbs)</p>
<p><strong>Team/Association:</strong> Godoi Jiu Jitsu</p>
<h3>Roberto Godoi Biography</h3>
<p>Roberto Godoi was born in Sao Paulo, Brazil. He started training Jiu Jitsu in 1988 with the late <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/marcelo-behring">Marcelo Behring</a>, one of the great Jiu Jitsu figures of the 1980&#8242;s. Godoi trained under the wild Behring for the duration of one year until Marcelo decided to move to Australia. He then began training with the founder of Companhia Atlética Paulista (Cia Paulista) Valdomiro Perez who was a brown belt under Behring at the time.</p>
<p>In 1992 Jorge Patino &#8220;Macaco&#8221; who was a school friend and neighbour of Godoi decided to join Perez&#8217;s BJJ academy also. Patino was a Rugby player who also practiced Judo while Godoi was already a purple belt at the time, with two Brazilian national titles under his belt.</p>
<p>In 1994 Roberto opened an affiliate school of the Cia Paulista in partnership with Valdomiro Perez and Jorge Patino. That partnership lasted for one year until Macaco and Godoi decided to cut their bond with the Cia Paulista. Roberto and Jorge perused their careers as athletes during the following year, both premiering in MMA, Macaco made a staggering total of 5 fights that year winning the belt of the &#8220;Circuito de Lutas&#8221; while Roberto Godoi also won 2 fights by submission in 1995.</p>
<p>On 1996 (a year after the break) they returned to teaching BJJ, opening their own gym, the Academia Macaco/Godoi. Their team was extremely successful for the following 4 years, winning 4 Paulista (Sao Paulo State) championships on a row, beating many of the strong competitors such as Ryan Gracie Academy, Alliance and Cia Paulista, a truly remarkable feature for such a fresh team. Their partnership seemed to work perfectly with both Jiu Jitsu men&#8217;s schedules, &#8220;Macaco&#8221; always competing elevating the academy&#8217;s name while also teaching a class, and Roberto Godoi taking his low key profile though being an immensely important figure teaching most of the athletes.</p>
<p>In 2001 the shock break up occurred, the two parted in less then amicable terms over money arrangements. Patino went on to open his &#8220;Macaco Gold Team&#8221; while Roberto opened the &#8220;Godoi Jiu Jitsu&#8221;. Many of the fighters followed Godoi, people like <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/marco-barbosa-facts-and-bio">Marco Barbosa</a>, <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/bruno-frazatto-bjj-fighter-wiki">Bruno Frazatto</a> and <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/roberto-tozi-wiki-bio">Roberto Tozi</a>. Jorge took offence on the fighters that did not follow his footsteps which aggravated further an already troubled relationship. The two coaches exchanged harsh words on many occasions. Godoi claimed that &#8220;Macaco&#8221; used to call him Master when they owned the Macaco/Godoi, while Patino replied &#8220;when we meet I knock him out and I offer him my hand for Godoi to get back up I will then ask him: Do you want my hand master?&#8221;. Godoi also stated on another interview: &#8220;what he has is envy, Macaco is acting like a betrayed woman&#8221;</p>
<p>With such grief being yelled from both sides a match between the two was inevitable, and it did happen. In jiu jitsu it happened twice, with one fight going to Macaco by advantages and the other going to Godoi by sweep on the final of the Sao Paulo State Championship. In 2006 the two were offered a large sum of money to fight in an MMA main event. The offer was accepted in a fight were Macaco TKO&#8217;ed Godoi on the second round.</p>
<p>Today Godoi does seminars throughout the World and has his own academy in Sao Paulo.</p>
