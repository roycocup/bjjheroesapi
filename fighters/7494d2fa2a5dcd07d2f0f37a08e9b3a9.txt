<p>Fernando Pontes, also known as Margarida by Jiu Jitsu fans all around the globe, is a former World BJJ CHampion in both his weight class and the open weight division. He is known for his aggressive style, constantly looking for submissions, and for his quarrels with a few of his instructors.</p>
<h2>Fernando Margarida in Detail</h2>
<p><strong>Nickname:</strong> Margarida means &#8220;Daisy&#8221; in Portuguese. The name was given when he first started training at <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/fabio-gurgel-wiki">Fabio Gurgel</a> and Fernando Yamasaki&#8217;s academy, it started when in Carnival season Fernando Pontes asked his dentist to put the elastic bands on the braces in different colours (in theme with the festive season). When he showed up at the gym, Fabio Gurgel thought it was so funny he named him “Margarida”.</p>
<p><strong>Lineage:</strong> Mitsuyo Maeda &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/carlos-gracie-sr-profile">Carlos Gracie</a> &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/helio-gracie">Helio Gracie</a> &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/rickson-gracie-facts-and-bio">Rickson Gracie</a> &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/marcelo-behring">Marcelo Behring</a> &gt; Waldomiro Perez &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/roberto-godoi">Roberto Godoi</a> &gt; Fernando Pontes &#8220;Margarida&#8221;</p>
<p><strong>Main Achievements (BJJ/MMA/Grappling):</strong></p>
<ul>
<li> 2x World Jiu Jitsu Champion (weight and Absolute Divisions 2001)</li>
<li>3x Pan American BJJ Champion (1999 purple, 2000, 2001 black)</li>
<li>Brazilian National Champion (2000)</li>
<li>2x World Silver Medalist (2000 weight and absolute)</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Favorite Technique:</strong> Cross choke, also known as Baseball Choke or &#8220;Alicate&#8221; in Portuguese (meaning Pliers choke).</p>
<p><strong>Weight Category:</strong> Medio Pesado (up to 194.5lbs).</p>
<p><strong>Most famous fight:</strong> against <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/saulo-ribeiro-facts-and-bio">Saulo Ribeiro</a> at the finals of his weight category in the World Championships of 2001, Saulo Ribeiro hadn’t lost a fight in 4 years. (See fight video at the bottom of this page).</p>
<h3>Fernando Pontes &#8220;Margarida&#8221; Biography</h3>
<p>Fernando “Margarida” Pontes was born in Sao Paulo, Brazil. Being raised in a family were almost everybody was a doctor, it was only natural that his parents would want young Fernando Pontes to pursue a career in medicine, fortunately for us BJJ lovers, that wasn&#8217;t what the future had installed for him. As a child his mother tried to make him do sports. She enrolled him in Basketball, however, due to his small stature, he felt like there wasn’t much future for himself in the tall men game, convincing his mother to take him off the team. Since &#8220;Margarida&#8221; was a little menace in the streets, always picking up fights with other kids, his mother decided to put him in a Brazilian Jiu Jitsu class, so he could release his anger. Fernando was only 13 when he stepped inside “Academia Formula” the academy of Fernando Yamasaki and <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/fabio-gurgel-wiki">Fabio Gurgel</a>, were he got his nickname (as mentioned above).</p>
<p>Fabio Gurgel soon left the academy to open up an academy of his own and Margarida continued his training under Yamasaki for the following 2 years (until he was 15 years old), about the same time he got his Blue Belt. He then moved to the United States (Washington) and trained with Mario Yamasaki (his former teacher&#8217;s brother) for 6 moths. Now competing mainly in the USA Pontes moved to California as that was where most tournaments were. In Cali he began training with Fabio Santos, and was awarded his Purple Belt by him, He also received his Brown Belt from Fábio and to this day, Santos is the man Fernando Margarida calls his master.</p>
<p>His training days in California were finished when his family moved on back to Sao Paulo. Margarida intended to return to the “Formula” academy of Fernando Yamasaki, but in an example of the &#8220;bad boy&#8221; image that has characterized Fernando&#8217;s career, he had an incident with the Doorman of the academy which ended in a fight and he was expelled. Margarida also spent some time training with <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/nino-schembri">Nino Shembri</a>, but ended up being asked to leave by Nino after a misunderstanding. (all this information is available at an interview given to Tatame Magazine in 2002)</p>
<p>He returned to the US where he also took time to train with his favourite fighter and the man who he always tried to mimic on the mats, <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/rickson-gracie-facts-and-bio">Rickson Gracie</a>. After this, another trip to Sao Paulo, Brazil to train with <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/roberto-tozi-wiki-bio">Roberto Godoi</a> and Otavio de Almeida, the men who awarded him his Black Belt. That year &#8220;Margarida&#8221; achieved silver at both his weight and the absolute weight division at the World Championships. The following year he won the Pan Americans, submitting his former teacher Fabio Gurgel (who had never been submitted in competition before), and won his weight and the Absolute weight div., at the Mundial (World Championships).</p>
<p>The following years have been full of ups and downs, with a venture in both the ADCC and in MMA with relative success. Many on internet forums argue that his drive for competition disappeared, mainly due to the “Playboy” status achieved by Fernando Margarida Pontes after reaching fame in the early 2000’s combined with the fact that Margarida is from a wealthy family and he didn&#8217;t need to compete for a living. BJJ Heroes doesn&#8217;t pass judgment, and hopefully Mr Fernando Pontes will find in his heart the will to return to the mats as he was one of the most entertaining fighters to watch in the sport.</p>
<p>Margarida is also famous for his famous citation, quotes many times in many Grappling Forums – <strong>“Jiu Jitsu is like tight jeans”</strong>. Check video also at the bottom of this page</p>
