<p>Tarsis Humphreys is a Brazilian Jiu Jitsu powerhouse and a top fighter in the black belt division since 2006. The Alliance team fighter was also the winner of the very first Professional Jiu Jitsu tournament (World Pro Cup) in both his weight and the Absolute (open weight) division back in 2009.<br />
.</p>
<h2>Tarsis Humphreys in Detail</h2>
<p><strong>Full Name:</strong> Tarsis Carvalho Humphreys<br />
.<br />
<strong>Lineage: </strong>Mitsuyo Maeda &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/carlos-gracie-sr-profile">Carlos Gracie</a> &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/helio-gracie">Helio Gracie</a> &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/rolls-gracie-profile">Rolls Gracie</a> &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/romero-jacare-cavalcanti-wiki">Romero Cavalcanti</a> &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/fabio-gurgel-wiki">Fábio Gurgel</a> &gt; Tarsis Humphreys<br />
.<br />
<strong>Main Achievements:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>World Champion (2010);</li>
<li>2x World Pro Cup Winner (2009 Weight and Absolute);</li>
<li>3x Brazilian National Champion (2006, 2009, 2010);</li>
<li>2x Pan American Champion (2006 Weight and Absolute);</li>
<li>World Silver Medallist (2009);</li>
<li>5x World Bronze Medallist (2004 Brown &#8211; Weight and Absolute, 2005 Brown &#8211; Weight, 2007 Black &#8211; Weight, 2008 Black &#8211; Weight);</li>
<li> Bronze Medallist at the ADCC Finals (2007);</li>
<li>2nd Place ADCC Brazilian Trials (2007)</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Weight Category:</strong> Meio Pesado (Medium-Heavyweight) 88kg-194lbs<br />
.<br />
<strong>Association/Team:</strong> Alliance</p>
<h3>Tarsis Humphreys Biography</h3>
<p>Tarsis was born on November 4th 1983 in Sao Paulo, Brazil. He started training Jiu Jitsu when he was around 13 years of age through a friend who already trained the martial art as a way to do a physical activity as he not like the more common sports, like soccer (Brazil’s biggest sport).</p>
<p>He was awarded his black belt when he was 23 years old after reaching the podium of the World Championships on two years straight as a brown belt. The first year as a Black Belt (2006) came to prove an excellent period in Tarsis’s career, winning the Pan American Championships in both his weight and the open weight (absolute) category.</p>
<p>In 2009 after winning both his weight class and the absolute division at the World Pro Cup finals in Abu Dhabi winning a total of 14 000 dollars, Tarsis told the press he would use that money to invest on tuition for a new profession, though maintaining that he would not leave Jiu Jitsu.</p>
