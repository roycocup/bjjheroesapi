This Brazilian born black belt is the head instructor of the renowned Paragon BJJ  academy in the United States having graduated some of the top athletes of today such as <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/jeff-glover">Jeff Glover</a> and Bill Cooper amongst many others<br />

<h3>Ricardo Miller in Detail</h3>
<p><strong>Full Name:</strong> Ricardo Miller Chagas Ribeiro<br />
.<br />
<strong>Meaning of Ricardo Miller&#8217;s Nickname:</strong> &#8220;Franjinha&#8221; means fringe. The name came about when Ricardo started training at Cavalcanti&#8217;s gym. Miller had straight hair and he would always have a bowl style haircut, which resembled a cartoon character famous in those days from the &#8220;Turma da Monica&#8221; comic books and TV show. That character was called Franjinha<br />
.<br />
<strong>Ricardo Franjinha Miller Lineage:</strong> Mitsuyo Maeda > <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/carlos-gracie-sr-profile">Carlos Gracie</a> > <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/helio-gracie">Helio Gracie</a> > <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/rolls-gracie-profile">Rolls Gracie</a> > <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/romero-jacare-cavalcanti-wiki">Romero Cavalcanti</a> > Ricardo Miller<br />
.<br />
<strong>Ricardo Franjinha Miller Main Titles:</strong> World Champion (1996 &#8211; Brown Belt Division); 5x Pan American Champion (2010 &#8211; Black Belt Senior 2 Weight and Absolute, 2007 /2006 &#8211; Black Belt Senior 1, 2003 &#8211; Black Belt Master); 2x World No Gi Champion (2008, 2007 &#8211; Black Belt Senior 1)<br />
.<br />
<strong>Ricardo Franjinha Miller Weight Division:</strong> Peso Médio (Middleweight)<br />
.<br />
<strong>Ricardo Franjinha Miller Academy/Association:</strong> Paragon BJJ</p>
<h2>Ricardo Franjinha Miller Biography </h2>
<p>Ricardo Miller was born on the 30th of June 1969 in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil.He grew up on one of the most famous burghs of Rio, the Leblon which neighbours Copacabana. </p>
<p>Ricardo is of American descent, his greatgrandfather was from the USA. It is said that his family owned a Mill a few generations ago, thus the name &#8220;Millers&#8221; which later turn into Miller. Another peculiarity of Ricardo Franjinha Miller is that unlike many of the athletes in BJJ today who had very humble upbringings, Miller came from a middle class family in Brazil and had a chance to study in a good school. He found Jiu Jitsu in his late teens (when he was 19) and straight away started training with his one and only mentor, <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/romero-jacare-cavalcanti-wiki">Romero Cavalcanti</a> at the Jacaré academy in Ipanema &#8211; Rio de Janeiro. </p>
<p>At Jacaré&#8217;s place Miller had the opportunity to train with and under many of the big names of his time. People like <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/fabio-gurgel-wiki">Fábio Gurgel</a>, Traven and &#8220;Telo&#8221; Mendes. All this hard training paid off in 1996 as &#8220;Franjinha&#8221; took gold at the Mundial (World Championships) in the meio-pesado brown belt division. He was awarded his black belt by master Romero Cavalcanti shortly after his World title (February 1997) combining a total of 8 years between the time he first stepped on a mat and his black belt ceremony.<br />
In 1997 Franjinha decided he needed a new challenge in life. Ricardo was already one of the coaches at the prestigious Alliance academy in Sao Paulo (Fabio Gurgel&#8217;s Headquarters), but after a brief stay in Santa Barbara, CA for the 1996 BJJ Pan American he fell in love with the place and moved to the US, seeing great potential for BJJ in the country. </p>
<p>In California Ricardo Miller founded the Paragon Academy, this academy has flourished throughout the last decade having become a testament to it&#8217;s name, a true model of excellence to all BJJ academies over the World, with great fighters coming out of it&#8217;s camp. Paragon today is famous for it&#8217;s technical prowess that has graduated names such as Jeff Glover, Bill Cooper and Tyrone Glover (who was Franjinha&#8217;s first black belt), but another of Paragon&#8217;s most successful competitors is also it&#8217;s founder, Miller is still a very active competitor in many top level events such as the Pan American the Nogi World Championships and many others.</p>
