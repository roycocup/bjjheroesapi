						</div><p><div id="attachment_611" class="wp-caption alignright" style="width: 213px"><a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/Jeff-Glover.jpg"><img src="http://www.bjjheroes.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/Jeff-Glover-203x300.jpg" alt="Jeff Glover BJJ" title="Jeff-Glover" width="203" height="300" class="size-medium wp-image-611" /></a><p class="wp-caption-text">Jeff Glover</p></div>Jeff Glover is a gifted American grappler from Paragon BJJ. This <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/ricardo-franjinha-miller">Ricardo Miller</a> Jiu Jitsu black belt has one of the most attractive fighting styles in Submission Wrestling and BJJ today, with his innovative deep half guard (check <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/jiu-jitsu-dvd-reviews/jeff-glover-deep-half-guard-dvd-review">Jeff Glover&#8217;s DVD</a> review)</a>. He was also made famous through his series of episodes called &#8220;<a href="http://gan.doubleclick.net/gan_click?lid=41000000032876194&#038;pubid=21000000000302733">Glover&#8217;s Travels</a>&#8221; where he travelled throughout the United States interviewing and training with some of the best coaches in the country.<br />
.<br />
<strong>Full Name:</strong> Jeffrey Dean Glover<br />
.<br />
<strong>Meaning of Jeff Glover&#8217;s Nickname:</strong> He is called &#8220;The Pipelayer&#8221; because he used to be a plumber.<br />
.<br />
<strong>Jeff Glover&#8217;s Lineage:</strong> Mitsuyo Maeda > <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/carlos-gracie-sr-profile">Carlos Gracie</a> > <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/helio-gracie">Helio Gracie</a> > <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/rolls-gracie-profile">Rolls Gracie</a> > <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/romero-jacare-cavalcanti-wiki">Romero Cavalcanti</a> > <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/ricardo-franjinha-miller">Ricardo Miller</a> > Jeff Glover<br />
.<br />
<strong>Jeff Glover&#8217;s Main Sporting Achievements:</strong> Mundial de No-Gi Champion (2007); 2x Pan American Champion (2006 and 2005 &#8211; brown belt divisions); 2x ADCC veteran; 15x Open Weight champion for different organizations throughout his career<br />
.<br />
<strong>Jeff Glover&#8217;s Weight Division:</strong> Pena (Featherweight) 70kg/154lbs<br />
.<br />
<strong>Jeff Glover&#8217;s Favourite Position/Technique:</strong> Deep Half Guard Sweep<br />
.<br />
<strong>Jeff Glover&#8217;s Team/Association:</strong> Paragon BJJ</p>
<h2>Jeff Glover&#8217;s Biography</h2>
<p>Jeff Glover was born on the 15th of April 1983 in East Los Angeles, CA &#8211; USA. And it would take him 16 years to find Jiu Jitsu, but when he did, it was love at first sight. Glover knew straight away this sport would change his life forever. </p>
<p>He started competing from the get go, and achieved success quickly. Even though Jeff Glover competed mainly at featherweight with some incursions in the Lightweight division, he always liked to stand the test at the absolute (open weight) division, something that not many featherweights dare enter. This display of confidence reaped it&#8217;s rewards as Glover stacked a fair amount of absolute medals and titles over the years. One of those titles won against Mike Fowler at the prestigious Grapplers Quest as a purple belt.<br />

<p>on the 8th of April 2006, just a few days short of his 23rd birthday, Jeff Glover received his black belt from the hands of <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/ricardo-franjinha-miller">Ricardo &#8220;Franjinha&#8221; Miller</a>, his first and only instructor, after winning his second Pan American gold medal in the brown belt division. Unfortunately for Jiu Jitsu, Glover has been struggling with some finger injuries for the last few years, which prevent him from fighting in Gi (kimono) tournaments, though he is still very active in Submission Wrestling tournaments.</p>
<p>In January 2010, Jeff Glover released a series of DVD&#8217;s about his pilgrimage around the US visiting all the main academies in the norther territory. This project was taken with great success by the BJJ community because of both it&#8217;s informative content and it&#8217;s entertainment value.
