<p>Bernardo Faria is another fantastic prospect out of the Alliance Jiu Jitsu Camp. This BJJ Black Belt as had a rampant career as of late, winning both the European Open, the Mundials and the Pan American in 2010.</p>
<h2>Bernardo faria in Detail</h2>
<p><strong>Full Name:</strong> Bernardo Rocha de Faria<br />
.<br />
<strong>Lineage:</strong> Mitsuyo Maeda &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/carlos-gracie-sr-profile">Carlos Gracie</a> &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/carlson-gracie-profile">Carlson Gracie</a> &gt; Ricardo Marques &gt; Bernardo Faria<br />
.<br />
<strong>Favourite Technique:</strong> “Wheel Sweep” from Deep Half Guard.<br />
.<br />
<strong>Main Achievements (Black Belt):</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>World Champion (2010);</li>
<li>2x Pan American Champion (2010 Heavyweight and Absolute);</li>
<li>Brazilian National Champion (2010 &#8211; Open Weight Division);</li>
<li>European Champion (closed the brackets with his coach and team mate <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/fabio-gurgel-wiki">Fabio Gurgel</a>);</li>
<li>World Bronze Medallist (2009);</li>
<li>European Bronze Medallist (2010 Open Weight/Absolute)</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Weight Category:</strong> Super-Heavyweight (100kg &#8211; 2021lbs) or Heavyweight (94kg &#8211; 201lbs)<br />
.<br />
<strong>Association/Team:</strong> Alliance (former BTT-Juiz de Fora/Academia Vida e Saúde)</p>
<h3>Bernardo Faria Biography</h3>
<p>Bernardo Faria was born in Juiz de Fora a small town on the State of Minas Gerais, Brazil.</p>
<p>He started training Jiu Jitsu in 2001 when he was 14 years old because one of his best friends (Pedro Cid) invited him to train at his gym. That gym was run by Ricardo Marques, a Carlson Gracie black belt who was then teaching under the Brazilian Top Team Banner.</p>
<p>Bernardo’s first major competition try out was the World Cup as a Juvenil in 2003, which he lost due to lack of experience, but he came back strong the next year (still as a Juvenile) and got the Silver Medal in his weight class and Bronze in the Open Weight division. He reached his blue belt that same year and there he stayed until 2005 getting 2 Silver Medals at the Mundial (World Championships) and one Bronze. He reached his Purple Belt in 2006 and that year he was Brazilian National Cup Champion and a World Champion in the Absolute Division among many other important trophies and so it didn&#8217;t come as a surprise when he was awarded his brown belt in 2007.</p>
<p>Although he wanted to try his luck at the Brazilian Nationals again in 2007, an appendicitis operation took him off the mats for a while and he was unable to compete, he did manage to compete in the World Championship reaching second place in his division. The next year a meniscus problem took him out of the World Championship. In 2009 after Bernardo received the Black Belt, he took a big step in his career. Understanding that he wanted to follow Jiu Jitsu as a career, he moved to Sao Paulo to join a bigger more structured team, the decision was taken his master Ricardo Marques’s consent and full support. He decided to join Alliance as he felt great admiration for <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/fabio-gurgel-wiki">Fabio Gurgel</a>, “The General” of the team’s Sao Paulo Head Quarters.</p>
