<p>Vinicius Magalhaes, also known as <em>Draculino</em>, is a &#8220;Carioca&#8221; (Rio de Janeiro native) and one of the World&#8217;s top instructors in Jiu Jitsu, having taught several top level black belt competitors throughout the years. Fighters such as <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/romulo-barral-wiki">Romulo Barral</a>, Samuel Braga, Marcelo Azevedo and many others. He is now teaching in the USA.<br />
.</p>
<h2>Vinicius Magalhaes in Detail</h2>
<p><strong>Full Name:</strong> Vinicius Bittencourt Almeida Magalhães.</p>
<p><strong>Nickname and Reason for Nickname:</strong> Draculino was a joke name given by Vinicius math’s teacher when he was still a child. It stands for something like “Little Dracula”.</p>
<p><strong>Lineage:</strong> Mitsuyo Maeda &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/carlos-gracie-sr-profile">Carlos Gracie Sr.</a> &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/carlos-carlinhos-gracie-jr">Carlos Gracie Junior</a> &gt;Vinicius Magalhães “Draculino”</p>
<p><strong>Main Achievements (BJJ):</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>3x Pan American Champion (Black Belt Division 1995, 1996, 1998);</li>
<li>Brazilian National Champion (Black Belt Division 1996);</li>
<li>2x World Silver Medallist (Black Belt Division 1996, 1998).</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Weight Division:</strong> Pluma (Super Featherweight) 64Kg-141Lbs</p>
<p><strong>Association/Team:</strong> Gracia-Barra.</p>
<h3>Draculino Magalhaes Biography</h3>
<p>Vinicius Magalhaes was born in Rio de Janeiro on the 30th of July 1971 in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. He started his Martial Arts career when he was 7 years old by training Judo, a sport he perused until he was an Orange Belt. His contact with Jiu Jitsu came later in his life (around 1884/85), Vinicius enjoyed surfing in the Quebra-Mar spot in Barra da Tijuca (Rio de Janeiro) and many of his surfing friends from that spot trained BJJ. One day he decided to take a class and soon got hooked to the Martial Art.</p>
<p>His first teacher was Totila Jordan “Pitoco”, one of Renzo Gracie’s best friends (Totila Jordan would later be the founder of the Infight Academy). “Draculino “ stayed with Master “Pitoco” for 11 months, until moving to the Gracie Jiu Jitsu Club (Later called Gracie Barra) by the hand of his friend and neighbour Ralph Gracie. The club was run by Jean Jacques Machado who was still a purple belt in the beginning.</p>
<p>He achieved his Purple Belt by the hands of <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/jean-jacques-machado-bjj-fighter-wiki">Jean Jacques</a> (who was a Brown Belt then). Soon after <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/carlos-carlinhos-gracie-jr">Carlos Gracie Junior</a> took over the academy, calling it Gracie-Barra and “Draculino” began his tuition with Carlos from there on.  He received his black belt in 1995 from the hands of the Gracie.</p>
<p>In 1996 Vinicius moved to Belo Horizonte in the State of Minas Gerais in Brazil, he opened one of the very first Gracie Barra academies outside of Rio de Janeiro. Vinicius Draculino also has a degree in Law, and worked as a lawyer for a short period. He soon understood that working as a lawyer was not what he wanted to pursue and started dedicating himself fully to Jiu Jitsu.</p>
<p>Showing the warrior spirit within him, Vinicius ventured in the world of MMA in 2003, even though he had been inactive as a competitor for a few years. He defeated Kleber Gaudino in a first round submission by arm triangle. He fought again in 2005, this time finding a much tougher challenge in Brazilian Top Team’s strong man Fabio Mello and was defeated by TKO. After he laid the base for his academy in Belo-Horizonte, he moved to the US to launch another Gracie-Barra base camp in Texas.</p>
<p>In 2010 he launched a website dedicated to BJJ techniques (link bellow)<br />
<a title="Draculino BJJ Training - An online jiu jitsu video and curriculum training site by Vinicius Magalhaes." href="http://www.draculinobjjtraining.com" target="_blank"><img src="http://www.draculinobjjtraining.com/assets/images/banners/Logo_288x83.jpg" border="0" alt="Draculino BJJ Training - An online jiu jitsu video and curriculum training site by Vinicius Magalhaes." width="288" height="83" /></a></p>
<p>Today Draculino has awarded over 100 black belts throughout his academies and is one of the best coaches in the business.<br />
