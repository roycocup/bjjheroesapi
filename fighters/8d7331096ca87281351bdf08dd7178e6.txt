<p>Alexandre Ribeiro also known as Xande is a Jiu Jitsu and Mixed Martial Arts (MMA) expert with one of the best BJJ resumes in the World. He is also the younger brother of one of Jiu Jitsu all time greats <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/saulo-ribeiro-facts-and-bio">Saulo Ribeiro</a>.</p>
<h2>Alexandre Ribeiro in Detail</h2>
<p><strong>Nickname:</strong> Xande is a common way of saying Alexandre in Brazil. The nickname was given by the Monteiro Brothers, Alexandre Ribeiro’s First instructors<br />
.<br />
<strong>Lineage:</strong> Mitsuyo Maeda > <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/carlos-gracie-sr-profile">Carlos Gracie</a> &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/helio-gracie">Helio Gracie</a> &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/royler-gracie-facts-and-bio">Royler Gracie</a> &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/saulo-ribeiro-facts-and-bio">Saulo Ribeiro</a> &gt; Alexandre Ribeiro<br />
.<br />
<strong>Weight Category:</strong> Heavy and Super Heavyweight<br />
.<br />
<strong>Main Achievements:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li> 6x World Black Belt Champion (2008 weight &amp; absolute, 2006 weight &amp; absolute, 2005)</li>
<li>4x Black Belt Pan American Champion (2006, 2005, 2001 absolute, 2002)</li>
<li>2x World Silver Medallist (2010, 2005)</li>
<li>ADCC Champion (2009, 2007)</li>
<li>ADCC Silver Medallist (2009 open weight)</li>
<li>ADCC Bronze Medallist (2005)</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Team/Association: </strong>Gracie Humaitá/ Jiu Jitsu University</p>
<h2>Xande Ribeiro Biography</h2>
<p>Xande Ribeiro was born January 20, 1981 in Manaus – Brazil. He started his Brazilian Jiu Jitsu tuition under the Monteiro Brothers School, who were a team under the guidance of Royler Gracie and the Famous Gracie Humaita, Alexandre was 10 at the time. He excelled in BJJ from the start just like his brother, another BJJ great, who goes by the name of <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/saulo-ribeiro-facts-and-bio">Saulo Ribeiro</a>. It is said that in his first 7 years of training he only lost 2 times in competition.</p>
<p>When he turned 18 he moved to Rio de Janeiro to study law, but he never stopped training and competing. He joined Gracie Humaita matrix academy ran by <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/royler-gracie-facts-and-bio">Royler Gracie</a> and in which his brother was a star pupil. Xande went on to win two world championships and Pan Americans during the the fist two years at the &#8220;Cidade Maravilhosa&#8221; (Rio de Janeiro).</p>
<p>On March 2001 he was awarded his well deserved Black Belt in Brazilian Jiu Jitsu by the hands of 6 time World BJJ Champion (and proud brother) Saulo Ribeiro.</p>
<p>His BJJ career has been plagued with injuries (specially in the first couple of years after winning his black belt), even though he managed to win the Pan American’s Absolute Division (Open weight) that year. He healed up and hit the ground running getting gold at his weight class (Heavy Weight) in 2004 at the Mundial (World Championships) and taking the bronze medal in the open weight &#8211; absolute &#8211; division.</p>
<p>In 2005 he struck gold again at the Pan Ams and World stage in the Absolute and Finished 2nd in his weight. He also had a good run at the ADCC (the biggest submission Wrestling tournament in the world) getting 3rd place in his division.</p>
<p>2006 had more of the same installed for Xande as he won the Pan American title and a World Cup title, as well as winning the Absolute Division and The Heavy Weight division. The following year came and Grappling&#8217;s greatest honour came with it as Xande was crowned winner of that year&#8217;s eddition of Abu Dhabi&#8217;s Combat Club (ADCC), the World&#8217;s greatest submission Wrestling competition.</p>
<p>2008 Another bull’s-eye at the Absolute division and his weigh at the World Jiu Jitsu Championships.</p>
<p>He now resides in the United States together with his brother Saulo Ribeiro. They opened an academy called University of Jiu Jitsu in California were he teaches amogst many others, former UFC Lightweight contender Diego Sanchez. Alexandre has also picked up MMA since then and is currently undefeated in the sport.</p>
