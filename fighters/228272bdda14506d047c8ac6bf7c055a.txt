<p>Rafael Mendes is a Brazilian Jiu Jitsu Black Belt from the Rio Claro team, Atos. Considered the king of the featherweight division by many BJJ enthusiasts, he is also one of the fighters that brought recognition to the (now) famous 50-50 Guard, the guard he used to de-throne the former BJJ featherweight world champion, Rubens Charles. Rafael Mendes is also the brother of another Jiu Jitsu personality, <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/guilherme-mendes-facts-and-bio">Guilherme Mendes</a>.<br />
.</p>
<h2>Rafael Mendes Details</h2>
<p><strong>Lineage:</strong> Mitsuyo Maeda &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/carlos-gracie-sr-profile">Carlos Gracie </a>&gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/carlson-gracie-profile">Carlson Gracie</a> &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/andre-pederneiras-bjj-and-mma-coach">Andre Pederneiras</a> &gt; <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/ramon-lemos-atos">Ramon Lemos</a> &gt; Rafael Mendes<br />
.<br />
<strong>Most Famous Fight:</strong> against <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/rubens-charles-cobrinha-bjj-fighter-wiki">Rubens Charles Cobrinha</a> in the final of ADCC 2009, a fight that went to several overtime extensions and lasted over 40 minutes.<br />
.<br />
<strong>Main Achievements (in BJJ/Grappling):</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>4x World Champion (2010 &#8211; black, 2008 &#8211; brown, 2007 &#8211; purple;  2006 &#8211; blue);</li>
<li>ADCC Champion (2010);</li>
<li>European Champion (2010);</li>
<li>2x Jiu Jitsu World Pro Cup Champion (2010, 2009);</li>
<li>Brazilian National Champion (2010)</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Weight Category:</strong> Peso Pena (Featherweight) 70kg &#8211; 154lbs (with Kimono)<br />
.<br />
<strong>Favorite technique:</strong> Sweep from 50-50 Guard<br />
.<br />
<strong>Affiliation/Team:</strong> Atos Jiu Jitsu.</p>
<h3>Rafael Mendes Biography</h3>
<p>Rafael was born in Rio Claro, a city in the state of Sao Paulo, Brazil. He and his brother <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/guilherme-mendes-facts-and-bio">Guilherme Mendes</a> (also a World Famous BJJ Fighter) both started their training through a cousin by the name of Thiago Mendes, who was a purple belt under <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/leonardo-santos-leo-bjj-fighter-wiki">Leonardo Santos</a>. A few months after their training they were advised by Thiago to train at the main academy led by Leonardo and <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/ramon-lemos-atos">Ramon Lemos</a> (The man who would later form the <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/tag/atos-jiu-jitsu">Atos Team</a>), as he saw a lot of potential in both of them. Being just 13 years old, and incredibly gifted, he picked up all the techniques with amazing ease, and started to train almost full time, with two training sessions per day.</p>
<p>It was also around this time that Rafael’s parent’s split, leaving both Rafael and Guilherme without a fatherly figure. Both brothers have come out on several occasions and said that Mr Lemos filled most of that void, teaching them not only BJJ but the moral values which built their character. Rafael was an amazing BJJ student, winning all World Championships from blue belt all the way to brown in consecutive years, achieving the bronze medal in his first year as a black belt (2009) and winning his first world gold medal as a black belt in 2010.</p>
<p>Rafael’s amazing rise to BJJ stardom came overnight, when he was just 17. Having never competed without a gi (kimono) before, Ramon Lemos enlisted the teenager Rafael in the ADCC Brazilian Trials, the hardest selective for the biggest Submission Wrestling event in the world (Abu Dhabi Combat Club – ADCC). Rafael made it to the final of the trials, losing a hard fight against <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/bruno-frazatto-bjj-fighter-wiki">Bruno Frazatto</a> (who would later become his friend at Atos).</p>
<p>Ramon Lemos awarded Rafael his Black Belt in late 2008, and straight away his first year in the big league was filled with success. Rafael won the Abu Dhabi Pro Challenge, one of the best and hardest competitions in the world. He also won the 2009 ADCC finals and placed 3rd in the Mundial&#8230; All finals of all 3 competitions were fought against <a href="http://www.bjjheroes.com/bjj-fighters/rubens-charles-cobrinha-bjj-fighter-wiki">Rubens Charles &#8220;Cobrinha&#8221;</a> (Considered the best fighter of 2009 by the Brazilian Press).</p>
